# Excellence Loop — Body of Knowledge

## Core Concepts
- **Quality Rubric**: A structured evaluation framework with defined criteria, scoring scales, and thresholds for pass/fail.
- **Iterative Refinement**: The practice of scoring, improving, and re-scoring until all criteria meet the threshold.
- **Excellence Threshold**: The minimum acceptable score for each criterion (typically 7/10) and overall (typically 8/10).
- **Diminishing Returns**: After 3 iterations, the improvement per iteration typically decreases; know when to stop.
- **Artifact Certification**: A formal stamp indicating the artifact has passed the excellence loop and is ready for delivery.
- **Calibration**: Periodically adjusting rubric criteria and weights based on real-world feedback and lessons learned.
- **Quality Debt**: The accumulated gap between current artifact quality and the excellence standard — must be tracked and reduced.

## Patterns & Practices
- **Score Before Ship**: No artifact leaves the session without at least one pass through the excellence loop.
- **Lowest Score First**: Address the weakest criteria first for maximum impact per iteration.
- **Evidence-Backed Scoring**: Every score must cite specific evidence — "readability: 6/10 because function names are abbreviated and non-descriptive."
- **Audience-Calibrated Rubrics**: Different audiences require different emphasis — executives care about clarity and actionability, engineers care about accuracy and completeness.
- **Iteration Budget**: Set a maximum number of iterations (typically 3) to prevent perfectionism paralysis.
- **Cross-Artifact Consistency**: Score artifacts in a deliverable set together to ensure consistent quality level.

## Tools & Technologies
- **Rubric Templates**: Markdown templates for each artifact type with pre-defined criteria and scales.
- **Scoring Dashboards**: Track quality scores across artifacts and sessions for trend analysis.
- **Peer Review Integration**: Feed excellence loop scores into code/document review processes.
- **Automation**: Lint rules, formatters, and CI checks that auto-score measurable criteria.

## References
- W. Edwards Deming, "Out of the Crisis" — The Plan-Do-Check-Act cycle applied to quality.
- ISO 25010 — Software product quality model with defined quality characteristics.
- Kaizen philosophy — Continuous improvement through small, iterative changes.
- Daniel Pink, "Drive" — Mastery as intrinsic motivation for quality work.
