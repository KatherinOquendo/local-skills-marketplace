- **Date:** March 2026
- **Classification:** Confidential

  - 72% — Reduction in Critical Incidents
- **One-line narrative below cards:** "A phased modernization that pays for itself in 14 months and positions Acme for the $340M digital banking opportunity."

**Speaker Notes:**
"Four numbers that summarize the business case. $1.8 million in annual savings, payback in 14 months, and a 72% reduction in the critical incidents that are costing you customer trust. Let me show you how we got here."

**Visual Direction:** High-impact data visualization. Each card is a dark rounded rectangle with the gold number prominently displayed. Clean white background. This is the slide people remember.

## Slide 3: The Problem — What's Costing You Today

**Layout:** Left: metrics table. Right: severity indicators.

**Content:**
- **Business Impact Table:**

  | Metric | Current | Target | Gap | Annual Impact |
  |--------|---------|--------|-----|---------------|
  | Manual reconciliation | 4,200 hrs/yr | 420 hrs/yr | -90% | $840K |
  | Customer onboarding | 12 days | 2 days | -83% | $520K |
  | Regulatory findings | 14 findings | 0 critical | -100% | $680K |
  | System downtime | 47 hrs/yr | <4 hrs/yr | -91% | $360K |

- **Total annual impact:** $2.4M
- **Pain severity badges:** 2 CRITICAL (red), 2 HIGH (orange)

**Speaker Notes:**
"These are not projections — these are your actual numbers from the FY2025 operational report. $2.4 million in quantifiable annual waste. Two items are critical: reconciliation errors delaying month-end by 5+ days, and customer data fragmented across 7 systems. Both are root-cause problems, not symptoms."

**Visual Direction:** Data-dense but clear. Use MetodologIA orange for HIGH severity, red for CRITICAL. No decoration — let the numbers speak.

## Slide 4: Cost of Inaction — The 4-Dimension View

**Layout:** 4 cards (2x2 grid) + consolidated total bar at bottom

**Content:**
- **4 Cost-of-Inaction Cards:**
  - Technical Debt: $3.25M (3yr) — Legacy maintenance, vendor lock-in, talent scarcity
  - Operational Waste: $4.68M (3yr) — Manual processes, FTE growth, error rates
  - Competitive Erosion: $1.97M (3yr) — Market share loss, customer churn, slow launches
  - Regulatory Exposure: $2.25M (3yr) — Audit findings, fines, compliance gaps
- **Bottom bar (red background):** "Total Cost of Inaction: $12.15M over 3 years | Burn Rate: $290K/month"

**Speaker Notes:**
"This is the anchor of the business case. If Acme does nothing — and I mean literally nothing changes — the cost is $12.15 million over 3 years across these four dimensions. That's $290,000 every month. And it compounds: by Year 3, you're burning $388K per month because regulatory requirements tighten, competitors accelerate, and legacy talent gets scarcer. The question isn't whether you can afford to modernize — it's whether you can afford not to."

**Visual Direction:** This is the emotional peak of the presentation. Red accents on the cards. The bottom total bar should be visually arresting. Use the burn rate as the anchoring number.

## Slide 5: Strategic Value — 4 Pillars

**Layout:** 4 pillar cards in a row, each with icon + metric + mechanism

**Content:**
- **Pillar 1: Cost Reduction** — $1.8M/yr savings. Automated reconciliation, unified data, self-service. Year 1: $720K.
- **Pillar 2: Revenue Acceleration** — $2.1M/yr incremental. Product launch 9mo to 6wks. Cross-sell. Year 1: $340K.
- **Pillar 3: Risk Mitigation** — 72% fewer incidents. Real-time monitoring, compliance automation. Year 1: $420K.
- **Pillar 4: Technical Modernization** — 99.97% uptime. API-first, strangler fig migration. 3x feature velocity.
- **Financial summary below:** NPV $3.1M | IRR 38% | Payback 14 months | TCO $4.2M

**Speaker Notes:**
"Four pillars of value, each independently justified. Cost reduction alone pays for the project. Revenue acceleration makes it strategic. Risk mitigation makes it urgent. And technical modernization makes it sustainable. The NPV is $3.1 million at a 12% discount rate — well above your hurdle rate. IRR of 38% — nearly triple the threshold."

**Visual Direction:** MetodologIA orange top borders on each card. Gold numbers for the metrics. Financial summary in a subtle dark bar at the bottom of the slide.

## Slide 6: Three Options — Your Choice

**Layout:** 3-column comparison table with recommended column highlighted

**Content:**
- **Comparison Matrix:**

  | Dimension | A: Do Nothing | B: Point Solutions | C: Phased Modernization |
  |-----------|--------------|-------------------|------------------------|
  | Investment | 0 FTE-months | ~72 FTE-months | ~216 FTE-months |
  | 3-Year TCO | $7.2M (waste) | $6.0M | $4.2M |
  | Payback | Never | 28 months | 14 months |
  | NPV | -$7.2M | $0.4M | $3.1M |
  | IRR | N/A | 12% | 38% |
  | Risk Reduction | 0% | 35% | 72% |
  | Tech Debt | +15%/yr | Stabilizes | -60% |

- **Column C highlighted** with gold border and "RECOMMENDED" badge
- **One-line verdict per option:**
  - A: "$12.15M cumulative loss. Regulatory risk escalates."
  - B: "Symptoms treated, root cause persists. 12% IRR barely meets hurdle rate."
  - C: "Lowest TCO. Highest ROI. Root causes eliminated. Phased de-risking."

**Speaker Notes:**
"Three options, transparent trade-offs. Option A costs you $12.15 million in inaction — it's the most expensive option, you're just paying differently. Option B addresses symptoms with point solutions but creates new integration complexity and barely meets your hurdle rate. Option C costs more upfront in effort but delivers the lowest 3-year TCO, the highest IRR, and eliminates root causes. Importantly, it's phased — each phase has a go/no-go gate. You're not committing to 216 FTE-months on day one; you're committing to 72 FTE-months for Phase 1 and a decision point."

**Visual Direction:** Option C column highlighted with gold background. Options A and B slightly greyed out. Clear visual hierarchy showing the recommended path.

## Slide 7: Investment Roadmap & Timeline

**Layout:** Top: Gantt-style timeline. Bottom: effort breakdown.

**Content:**
- **Gantt Chart (horizontal bars):**
  - Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-6, 72 FTE-months) — Gate 1 milestone
  - Phase 2: Migration (Months 7-12, 96 FTE-months) — Gate 2 milestone
  - Phase 3: Optimization (Months 13-18, 48 FTE-months) — Gate 3 milestone
  - Breakeven marker at Month 14

- **Effort Breakdown (horizontal stacked bar or small table):**
  - Architecture & Development: 130 FTE-months (60%)
  - Quality & Testing: 32 FTE-months (15%)
  - Infrastructure & DevOps: 22 FTE-months (10%)
  - Project Management: 16 FTE-months (7%)
  - Change Management: 16 FTE-months (7%)
  - **Total: 216 FTE-months**

- **Risk-Adjusted Timeline:**
  - Best case: 15 months (20% probability)
  - Base case: 18 months (60% probability)
  - Worst case: 22 months (20% probability, NPV still $2.1M)

- **Disclaimer footnote:** "Effort in FTE-months. Commercial terms subject to formal proposal."

**Speaker Notes:**
"Three phases, three gates. Phase 1 is 6 months and 72 FTE-months — this is where you validate the business case in production. If Phase 1 doesn't deliver $720K in validated savings, you have a decision point. Phase 2 is the heavy lift: core migration and compliance automation. Phase 3 is optimization and new product capability. Even in the worst-case scenario of 22 months, NPV remains $2.1 million — the business case is robust across scenarios."

**Visual Direction:** Gantt bars in MetodologIA orange. Gate milestones as gold diamonds. Breakeven marker in gold with a flag. Clean, professional timeline that shows phased commitment.

## Slide 8: Call to Action

**Layout:** Dark background (#1A1A2E), centered content, high-impact

**Content:**
- **Header:** "Decision Request"
- **Ask Box (centered, gold border):**
  - Approach: Option C — Phased Modernization
  - Effort: 216 FTE-months over 18 months
  - Start: April 2026
  - First Gate: Month 6 (go/no-go based on validated savings)
- **Decision Deadline (large, gold text):** March 28, 2026
- **Delay Cost (orange text):** "Every month of delay costs $290K"
- **Next Steps (4 items, bottom):**
  1. Week 1: Kick-off and team onboarding
  2. Week 2: Architecture design sessions
  3. Week 3: Sprint 1 begins
  4. Week 4: First stakeholder demo

**Speaker Notes:**
"We are asking for a decision by March 28. The ask is to approve the phased modernization approach — 216 FTE-months over 18 months, starting April 2026. The first gate is at Month 6 — that's where you validate the business case in production before committing to Phase 2. Every month of delay costs $290,000 in the compounding effects we showed earlier. We have alignment from the CTO and business sponsor. We need CFO budget approval and steering committee governance sign-off. Questions?"

**Visual Direction:** This is the closing slide — it must feel decisive. Dark background, gold decision deadline, orange delay cost. The contrast between the dark background and gold/orange text creates urgency without being aggressive. No clutter — just the ask, the deadline, and the cost of waiting.

## Presentation Notes

| Aspect | Guidance |
|--------|---------|
| **Duration** | 25 minutes presentation + 15 minutes Q&A |
| **Slide pacing** | Slides 1-2: 3 min (setup). Slides 3-4: 8 min (problem/urgency). Slides 5-6: 8 min (value/options). Slides 7-8: 6 min (investment/ask). |
| **Anchor technique** | Always reference Cost of Inaction ($12.15M) before discussing investment (216 FTE-months). Loss aversion drives decision. |
| **If pushed on price** | "Effort is 216 FTE-months. Commercial terms are in the formal proposal. Today's focus is the business case and approach." |
| **If asked about competitor** | "We've analyzed the competitive landscape. Two fintechs launched in the region in 2025. Each month of delay widens the gap." |
| **Handoff** | Leave behind: printed Slide 2 (Hero KPIs) + Slide 4 (Cost of Inaction). These are the two slides that drive internal conversations. |
