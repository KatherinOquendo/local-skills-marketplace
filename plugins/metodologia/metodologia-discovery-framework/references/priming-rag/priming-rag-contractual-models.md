# Standard Engagement & Contractual Models — Priming RAG

## Insights

Engagement models define how services are scoped, priced, delivered, and governed. During discovery, understanding the target engagement model is critical for accurate cost estimation, risk assessment, and roadmap design. The commercial model skill uses these patterns to structure deal proposals. This reference covers publicly known contractual patterns — no proprietary pricing or rate information.

## Key Points

- T&M (Time & Materials) is the most common model for consulting and software development
- Fixed Price requires well-defined scope — high risk for both parties if requirements are unclear
- Outcome-based models align incentives but require measurable KPIs
- Hybrid models combine elements to balance risk and flexibility
- Staff augmentation is the simplest model but offers least strategic value

## Content

### Core Engagement Models

#### 1. Time & Materials (T&M)

**Structure:** Client pays for hours/days worked at agreed rates per role.
**Risk profile:** Client bears scope risk; provider bears utilization risk.
**Best for:** Exploratory work, evolving requirements, R&D, innovation projects.
**Discovery implications:**
- Cost estimation produces FTE-months with rate ranges
- Scope flexibility built into roadmap
- Change management is lightweight (backlog grooming)
- Risk mitigation: velocity tracking, burn-rate monitoring

**Variants:**
- **T&M with cap:** Maximum budget ceiling with early warning at 80%
- **T&M with target:** Shared savings/penalties around target budget
- **Managed T&M:** Provider manages capacity; client approves scope only

#### 2. Fixed Price (FP)

**Structure:** Client pays agreed total for defined deliverables and scope.
**Risk profile:** Provider bears scope risk; client bears opportunity cost of rigidity.
**Best for:** Well-defined projects, compliance/regulatory, known technology, repeatable work.
**Discovery implications:**
- Scope must be exhaustively defined (functional spec is critical)
- Change request process required in contract
- Risk premium typically 15-30% built into estimate
- Contingency buffer: 10-20% for unknowns

**Variants:**
- **Fixed Price per phase:** Phased commitment reduces risk
- **Fixed Price with change orders:** Formal change management
- **Not-to-exceed:** Fixed ceiling with T&M underneath

#### 3. Staff Augmentation

**Structure:** Provider supplies qualified professionals to work under client direction.
**Risk profile:** Client bears all delivery risk; provider bears talent risk.
**Best for:** Temporary capacity needs, specialized skills, team scaling.
**Discovery implications:**
- Talent gap analysis is primary discovery output
- Skills matrix and profiling are key deliverables
- Minimal architecture/design work in discovery
- Focus on onboarding plan and knowledge transfer

**Variants:**
- **Individual augmentation:** Single specialist placements
- **Team augmentation:** Entire squad/pod placement
- **Managed team:** Provider manages team performance

#### 4. Outcome-Based / Value-Based

**Structure:** Payment tied to achievement of measurable business outcomes.
**Risk profile:** Shared — both parties invested in success.
**Best for:** Transformation programs, process optimization, measurable KPI improvement.
**Discovery implications:**
- Baseline metrics must be established (AS-IS measurement)
- Success criteria and KPIs defined during discovery
- Measurement methodology agreed before engagement
- Risk/reward sharing model designed in commercial model phase

**Variants:**
- **Gain sharing:** Provider receives percentage of measured savings
- **Success fee:** Bonus on achievement of milestones
- **Pay-per-outcome:** Payment per unit of delivered value

#### 5. Managed Services

**Structure:** Provider takes operational responsibility for defined services with SLAs.
**Risk profile:** Provider bears operational risk within SLA boundaries.
**Best for:** Ongoing operations, infrastructure management, application support.
**Discovery implications:**
- SLA definition is critical discovery output
- Operational runbooks and knowledge base required
- Transition plan from project to operations
- Cost model: monthly recurring vs project-based

### Hybrid Models

#### T&M + Fixed Price Hybrid

**Structure:** Discovery and early phases in T&M; execution phases in Fixed Price once scope is clear.
**When to recommend:** Client wants cost certainty but scope is initially unclear. Discovery in T&M produces the spec that enables Fixed Price execution.

#### Staffing + Managed Outcome

**Structure:** Team augmentation for capacity with outcome-based bonus for achieving targets.
**When to recommend:** Client needs hands AND results. Aligns incentives beyond just filling seats.

#### Phased Commitment

**Structure:** Each phase is independently scoped and contracted. Next phase depends on current phase results.
**When to recommend:** High uncertainty, new client relationship, transformational programs. Allows course correction between phases.

### Cost Estimation Principles

These principles apply across all engagement models during discovery:

1. **Costear != Cobrar** — Cost identification is disconnected from pricing/revenue
2. **Drivers, not prices** — Output cost drivers, inductors, and magnitude indicators
3. **5% innovation margin** — Every magnitude includes continuous improvement allocation
4. **Three-point estimation** — Optimistic, most likely, pessimistic for all estimates
5. **Confidence bands** — Express uncertainty explicitly (e.g., +/-30% at Phase 3, +/-15% at Phase 5)
6. **Role-based sizing** — Express effort in FTE-months by role, not absolute cost
7. **Contingency transparency** — Separate base estimate from contingency allocation

### Governance by Engagement Model

| Model | Reporting Cadence | Key Ceremonies | Escalation Path |
|-------|------------------|----------------|-----------------|
| T&M | Weekly burn-rate, biweekly demos | Sprint reviews, retrospectives | PM → Delivery Lead → Account |
| Fixed Price | Milestone-based, monthly status | Gate reviews, change control board | PM → PMO → Steering Committee |
| Staff Aug | Monthly utilization, quarterly reviews | Performance reviews, 1:1s | Team Lead → HR → Account |
| Outcome-Based | KPI dashboards (real-time), quarterly business reviews | Outcome reviews, calibration sessions | Delivery Lead → Joint Steering |
| Managed Services | SLA dashboards (real-time), monthly service reviews | Incident reviews, capacity planning | Service Manager → Account |
