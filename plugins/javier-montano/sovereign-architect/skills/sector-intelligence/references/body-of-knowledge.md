# Sector Intelligence — Body of Knowledge

## Core Concepts

1. **Regulatory Landscape**: The set of laws, standards, and guidelines that constrain technology decisions in a specific sector. Compliance is an architectural requirement, not an afterthought.
2. **Digital Maturity Benchmarking**: Comparing an organization's technology capabilities against sector peers and leaders. Provides context for what "good" looks like in this industry.
3. **Sector-Specific NFRs**: Non-functional requirements that are standard in specific industries. Banking: sub-second transaction processing. Healthcare: HIPAA-grade encryption. Retail: Black Friday scale elasticity.
4. **Technology Adoption Curves**: How quickly specific technologies are adopted within a sector. Enterprise banking adopts differently than fintech startups.
5. **Competitive Technology Intelligence**: Understanding what technology investments competitors and leaders are making. Informs strategic positioning and build-vs-buy decisions.
6. **Regulatory Horizon Scanning**: Monitoring upcoming regulatory changes that will affect technology decisions. Proactive compliance is cheaper than reactive remediation.

## Patterns

1. **Regulation-to-Architecture Mapping**: Systematically converting regulatory requirements into architectural constraints, patterns, and quality attributes.
2. **Sector Maturity Matrix**: Assessing organizational maturity against sector-specific dimensions (automation, cloud adoption, data analytics, security posture).
3. **Technology Radar (Sector-Specific)**: Adapting ThoughtWorks' technology radar concept to sector-specific contexts, assessing technologies by sector applicability.
4. **Constraint-Led Design**: Using sector constraints as primary design drivers rather than treating them as afterthought compliance checks.

## Tools

1. **PESTEL Analysis**: Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Environmental, Legal framework for sector analysis.
2. **Porter's Five Forces**: Competitive dynamics analysis applied to technology strategy.
3. **Gartner Hype Cycle**: Technology maturity assessment with sector-specific overlays.
4. **Regulatory Databases**: Sector-specific regulation catalogs (NIST for cybersecurity, PCI Council for payments).
