# Workshop Design — Body of Knowledge

## Core Concepts

1. **Event Storming**: Alberto Brandolini's collaborative technique for exploring complex business domains through domain events, commands, aggregates, and policies on an unlimited modeling surface.
2. **Impact Mapping**: Gojko Adzic's strategic planning technique that connects business goals to deliverables through actors and impacts, preventing feature factories.
3. **Design Sprint**: Google Ventures' 5-day process for answering critical questions through prototyping and testing. Compressed cycle from problem to validated solution.
4. **Diverge-Converge Pattern**: The fundamental workshop rhythm. Diverge to generate options (brainstorming, exploration). Converge to make decisions (voting, prioritization).
5. **Facilitation Design**: The art of designing structured interactions that extract group intelligence. Good facilitation is invisible — participants focus on content, not process.
6. **Time-Boxing**: Fixed-duration activity blocks that force prioritization and prevent tangential exploration from consuming the entire session.

## Patterns

1. **Big Picture Event Storming**: Full-day workshop mapping the entire business domain through events, hotspots, and bounded contexts. Best for domain discovery.
2. **Design Studio**: Rapid sketching technique where participants individually sketch solutions, present, critique, and iterate. Generates diverse ideas quickly.
3. **Dot Voting**: Democratic prioritization technique. Each participant gets N votes (dots) to allocate across options. Simple, fast, and transparent.
4. **Architecture Kata**: Practice format where small teams design architectures for given constraints within time-boxes, then present and critique.

## Tools

1. **Miro/Mural**: Digital whiteboard platforms for remote or hybrid workshop facilitation.
2. **Liberating Structures**: A menu of 33 microstructures for facilitating group interaction.
3. **Event Storming Supplies**: Unlimited paper roll, colored sticky notes (specific colors for events, commands, aggregates, policies), markers.
4. **Timer Apps**: Visible countdown timers for maintaining time-box discipline.
