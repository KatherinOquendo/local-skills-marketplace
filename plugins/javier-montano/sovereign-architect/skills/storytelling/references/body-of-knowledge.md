# Storytelling — Body of Knowledge

## Core Concepts

1. **Narrative Arc**: The structural progression of a story through setup, rising action, climax, and resolution. Applies to technical deliverables as effectively as to fiction.
2. **Opening Hook**: The first 1-2 sentences that capture attention. In technical storytelling, this is often a specific, surprising data point or a vivid scenario.
3. **Dramatic Tension**: The gap between the current state and the desired state. Tension drives the reader forward, seeking resolution.
4. **Evidence-Narrative Integration**: The practice of embedding data within story elements rather than separating "the story" from "the data."
5. **Memorability**: The ability of the audience to recall and retell the core message. Stories are 22x more memorable than facts alone.
6. **Resolution and Call to Action**: The ending must deliver both emotional closure (the tension is resolved) and practical guidance (here is what to do next).

## Patterns

1. **Three-Act Structure**: Setup (context + problem), Confrontation (analysis + options), Resolution (recommendation + path forward). The most versatile narrative structure.
2. **Hero's Journey (Simplified)**: The team/organization starts in an ordinary world, encounters a challenge, gains new insight, and returns transformed with a solution.
3. **Problem-Solution-Result**: Direct structure for case studies and assessments. State the problem vividly, present the solution with rationale, show the projected result.
4. **Contrast Narrative**: Alternating between "what is" and "what could be" to build tension and motivation throughout the deliverable.

## Tools

1. **Story Canvas**: A visual template for planning narrative elements (protagonist, conflict, evidence, resolution) before writing.
2. **Sparkline Framework (Duarte)**: Alternating between "what is" and "what could be" to create a persuasive presentation structure.
3. **Mermaid Journey Maps**: Visual representation of the narrative arc with key moments marked.
4. **Storyboard Templates**: Panel-by-panel planning for the sequence of information delivery.
