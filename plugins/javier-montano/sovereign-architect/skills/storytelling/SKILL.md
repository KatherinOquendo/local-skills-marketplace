# Storytelling

Applies narrative arc design to technical deliverables, transforming collections of
findings into coherent stories that build understanding, create urgency, and drive
decisions through structured dramatic progression.

## Guiding Principle

> *"Data informs. Stories persuade. The best technical deliverables do both by weaving evidence into narrative."*

## Procedure

### Step 1 — Story Elements Extraction

1. Identify the protagonist (who benefits or is affected).
2. Define the conflict (the problem, gap, or tension that must be resolved).
3. Catalog the evidence (data points, findings, metrics) that will serve the narrative.
4. Determine the desired resolution (the recommendation or decision).

### Step 2 — Arc Design

1. Choose the narrative structure: three-act, hero's journey, or problem-solution-result.
2. Design the opening hook: a concrete, specific moment that captures attention.
3. Structure the rising action: build complexity and tension through layered findings.
4. Craft the climax: the moment where the key insight or recommendation lands.

### Step 3 — Evidence Integration

1. Place data at emotionally resonant points in the narrative, not in isolation.
2. Use specific examples and scenarios instead of abstract generalizations.
3. Apply contrast (before/after, with/without) to make evidence tangible.
4. Ensure every data point serves the narrative — remove orphan facts.

### Step 4 — Narrative Polish

1. Verify that the story flows logically without requiring backtracking.
2. Check pacing: alternating between tension and relief maintains engagement.
3. Ensure the ending delivers both closure and a clear call to action.
4. Test: can someone retell the core story from memory after one reading?

## Quality Criteria

- The narrative has a clear protagonist, conflict, and resolution.
- Evidence is woven into the story, not bolted on as appendices.
- The opening hook captures attention within the first two sentences.
- A reader can retell the core message from memory after one reading.

## Anti-Patterns

- Presenting findings as a flat list without narrative structure.
- Using story elements that distract from or contradict the evidence.
- Creating artificial drama that undermines credibility.
- Telling the story chronologically when the audience needs the conclusion first.
