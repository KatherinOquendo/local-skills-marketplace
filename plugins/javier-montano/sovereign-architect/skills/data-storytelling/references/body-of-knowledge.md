# Data Storytelling — Body of Knowledge

## Core Concepts

1. **Anchor Stat**: The single most impactful data point that reframes the audience's understanding. Opens the data story and sets the tone for everything that follows.
2. **Comparison Framing**: The practice of presenting data alongside a reference point (baseline, benchmark, trend) to create meaning. A number in isolation communicates nothing.
3. **Magnitude Communication**: Strategies for making large or abstract numbers comprehensible. "5 million transactions" becomes "170 transactions every second, day and night."
4. **Data-Ink Ratio**: Edward Tufte's principle that every element in a visualization should serve the data. Remove everything that does not contribute to understanding.
5. **Progressive Disclosure of Data**: Layering data presentation from headline metric to supporting detail to raw evidence, allowing the audience to choose their depth.
6. **Confidence Communication**: Honestly representing data uncertainty through ranges, confidence intervals, and explicit assumption disclosure.

## Patterns

1. **Before-After Framing**: The most intuitive comparison. Show the baseline, then the change. The gap between them is the story.
2. **Trend + Inflection Point**: Show a time series and highlight the moment something changed. The inflection point is where the story lives.
3. **Benchmark Comparison**: Position internal metrics against industry benchmarks or peer organizations to create context and urgency.
4. **Unit Conversion for Impact**: Converting abstract metrics to relatable units. "99.9% uptime" becomes "8.7 hours of downtime per year."

## Tools

1. **Mermaid Charts**: Embedded Markdown charts for basic data visualization in documents.
2. **Tufte's Principles**: Guidance on data-ink ratio, chart junk elimination, and sparklines.
3. **Storytelling with Data Framework (Knaflic)**: Six-step process for transforming data into visual stories.
4. **Annotation Libraries**: Tools for adding insight callouts directly to data visualizations.
