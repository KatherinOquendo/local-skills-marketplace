# Risk Scorer — Body of Knowledge

## Core Concepts
- **Risk = Severity x Likelihood**: The fundamental equation for risk quantification `[HECHO]`
- **Risk appetite**: The level of risk an organization is willing to accept; varies by context
- **Risk treatment options**: Accept (live with it), mitigate (reduce impact/likelihood), transfer (insurance/outsource), avoid (eliminate the activity)
- **Residual risk**: The risk remaining after mitigations are applied; must still be within appetite
- **Risk velocity**: How quickly a risk can materialize and impact the system; high-velocity risks need faster response
- **Black swan events**: Low-probability, high-impact events that are often overlooked in standard risk assessment

## Patterns & Practices
- **Risk matrix (5x5)**: Standard grid mapping severity against likelihood for visual prioritization
- **FAIR (Factor Analysis of Information Risk)**: Quantitative risk analysis framework using monetary loss estimation
- **Bow-tie analysis**: Combines fault tree (causes) and event tree (consequences) around a risk event
- **Risk register**: Living document tracking identified risks, scores, owners, mitigations, and review dates
- **Pre-mortem technique**: Imagine the project has failed and work backward to identify what went wrong

## Tools & Technologies
- **Risk frameworks**: NIST RMF, ISO 31000, FAIR
- **Threat modeling**: STRIDE, PASTA, Attack Trees
- **Visualization**: Risk heatmaps, radar charts, trend graphs
- **Tracking**: Risk registers in spreadsheets, Jira risk issue types, dedicated GRC tools
