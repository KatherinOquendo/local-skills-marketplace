# Executive Pitch

Transforms technical analysis into executive-ready business cases that connect
engineering decisions to business outcomes through structured value pillars and ROI frameworks.

## Guiding Principle

> *"Executives do not buy technology. They buy outcomes. Frame every recommendation as a business result."*

## Procedure

### Step 1 — Audience and Stakes Analysis

1. Identify the decision-makers, their priorities, and their risk tolerance.
2. Map the organizational pain points the recommendation addresses.
3. Determine the decision timeline and competing initiatives.
4. Establish the language and metrics that resonate with this audience.

### Step 2 — Value Pillar Construction

1. Define 3-5 value pillars that connect the technical solution to business outcomes.
2. For each pillar, articulate the before/after state with measurable impact.
3. Support each pillar with evidence: benchmarks, case studies, or proof-of-concept results.
4. Prioritize pillars by audience relevance and defensibility.

### Step 3 — ROI and Investment Framework

1. Quantify the investment required (FTE-months, infrastructure, licensing).
2. Project returns across multiple dimensions: revenue, cost savings, risk reduction.
3. Calculate payback period and 3-year net value.
4. Include a confidence assessment for each projection.

### Step 4 — Narrative Assembly

1. Open with a one-paragraph executive summary (problem, solution, impact).
2. Present value pillars with supporting evidence.
3. Include a visual investment/return timeline.
4. Close with a clear ask and decision framework.

## Quality Criteria

- Executive summary is self-contained and under 200 words.
- Every value claim has quantified impact and an evidence tag.
- ROI projections include confidence levels and assumption disclosure.
- The pitch answers "why now" and "why this approach" explicitly.

## Anti-Patterns

- Leading with technology features instead of business outcomes.
- Presenting ROI without disclosing assumptions or confidence levels.
- Using technical jargon that alienates the target audience.
- Omitting the cost of inaction as a comparison baseline.
