# Commercial Model — Body of Knowledge

## Core Concepts

1. **Total Cost of Ownership (TCO)**: The complete financial impact of a technology decision across its full lifecycle, including acquisition, operation, maintenance, and decommissioning.
2. **Build vs Buy Analysis**: A structured decision framework comparing in-house development against commercial or open-source alternatives across cost, time, risk, and strategic dimensions.
3. **Value Capture**: How a technology investment translates into business outcomes — revenue generation, cost avoidance, risk reduction, or capability enablement.
4. **Switching Cost**: The total expense (financial, technical, organizational) of migrating away from a chosen solution. A key factor in vendor lock-in assessment.
5. **Core vs Context**: Wardley's distinction between activities that differentiate the business (core) and those that are necessary but not differentiating (context). Build core, buy context.
6. **Licensing Models**: The spectrum from perpetual licenses through subscription (SaaS) to open-source with commercial support, each with different cost profiles and risk characteristics.

## Patterns

1. **Wardley Mapping for Build/Buy**: Using value chain evolution to determine which components should be built (genesis/custom), configured (product), or consumed (commodity).
2. **Three-Horizon TCO**: Modeling costs across immediate (0-1 year), medium (1-3 year), and strategic (3-5 year) horizons to capture lifecycle dynamics.
3. **Reversibility Premium**: Assigning higher value to options that preserve the ability to change direction, even at higher short-term cost.
4. **Dual-Track Evaluation**: Running a lightweight proof-of-concept for the top two options before committing, to validate assumptions with evidence.

## Tools

1. **Wardley Maps**: Visual strategy tool for mapping value chains and technology evolution stages.
2. **Business Model Canvas**: Framework for articulating how value is created, delivered, and captured.
3. **Monte Carlo Simulation**: Probabilistic modeling for TCO projections when key variables are uncertain.
4. **RICE Scoring**: Reach, Impact, Confidence, Effort — a prioritization framework for comparing options.
