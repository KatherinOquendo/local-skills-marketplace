# Technical Writing — Body of Knowledge

## Core Concepts

1. **Progressive Disclosure**: Layering information so readers access increasing detail on demand. Level 1: TL;DR. Level 2: Section summaries. Level 3: Full detail with evidence.
2. **Evidence Attribution**: Tagging every assertion with its source type ([CODE], [DOC], [INFERENCE], [ASSUMPTION]) to establish trust and enable verification.
3. **Claim-Evidence-Implication**: The structural unit of technical writing. State what is true, show why it is true, then explain what it means for the reader.
4. **Terminology Precision**: Using domain terms consistently with defined meanings. Ambiguous terms are defined at first use and maintained in a glossary.
5. **Information Hierarchy**: The structural organization of content that supports both linear reading (narrative flow) and random access (reference lookup).
6. **Reading Context Awareness**: Designing documents for how they will be consumed — deep study, quick reference, decision support, or handover.

## Patterns

1. **TL;DR First**: Every document and every major section opens with a self-contained summary. Readers who stop at the TL;DR should still gain value.
2. **Inverted Pyramid**: Most important information first, supporting detail follows. Borrowed from journalism and adapted for technical content.
3. **Decision Table Format**: When multiple options or conditions exist, tables communicate faster and more accurately than prose.
4. **Visual-Verbal Pairing**: Complex concepts explained with both a diagram and a prose description. Either should stand alone; together they reinforce understanding.

## Tools

1. **Markdown**: The lingua franca of technical documentation. Supports progressive disclosure through heading hierarchy.
2. **Mermaid.js**: Diagram-as-code embedded in Markdown for architecture, sequence, and flow diagrams.
3. **Vale/Textlint**: Automated prose linting for consistency, terminology, and style guide enforcement.
4. **Diátaxis Framework**: Documentation structure framework with four quadrants: tutorials, how-to guides, reference, explanation.
