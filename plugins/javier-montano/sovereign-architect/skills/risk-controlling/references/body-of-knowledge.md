# Risk Controlling — Body of Knowledge

## Core Concepts

1. **Risk Exposure**: The product of probability and impact, providing a single comparable metric across different risk types.
2. **Risk Appetite**: The level of risk an organization is willing to accept in pursuit of its objectives. Defines the acceptance threshold.
3. **Cascading Failure**: When one risk materializing triggers a chain of related risks. Identifying cascades requires analyzing risk interdependencies.
4. **Early Warning Indicator**: A measurable signal that a risk is becoming more likely or its impact is increasing. Enables proactive response.
5. **Risk Response Strategy**: Accept (absorb), Mitigate (reduce probability or impact), Transfer (insurance, outsourcing), Avoid (eliminate the cause).
6. **Residual Risk**: The risk remaining after mitigation strategies are applied. Must be within the organization's risk appetite.

## Patterns

1. **Pre-Mortem Analysis**: Imagining the project has failed and working backward to identify causes. More effective than traditional brainstorming for risk identification.
2. **Risk Dependency Matrix**: Mapping which risks trigger or amplify other risks. Reveals hidden clusters and cascading scenarios.
3. **Monte Carlo Risk Simulation**: Probabilistic modeling of project outcomes based on risk distributions. Produces confidence intervals for timelines and costs.
4. **Risk-Based Testing**: Prioritizing testing effort based on risk exposure. High-risk areas receive deeper testing coverage.

## Tools

1. **Risk Heat Map**: 5x5 probability/impact matrix with color-coded zones (green, yellow, orange, red).
2. **Bow-Tie Diagram**: Visualization showing causes on the left, the risk event in the center, and consequences on the right, with barriers on both sides.
3. **FMEA (Failure Mode and Effects Analysis)**: Structured technique for identifying failure modes, their causes, and effects.
4. **Risk Burndown Chart**: Tracking aggregate risk exposure over time to visualize whether risk is decreasing as the project progresses.
