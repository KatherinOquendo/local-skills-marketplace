# Guide Generation Patterns — Reference for generate-teacher-guide

> DUA principles, rubric patterns, weekly planning template extracted from SOAP references.

## DUA — 3 Principles Summary

### Principle 1: Multiple Means of Representation (the WHAT)

Provide content in multiple formats so all students can perceive and comprehend it.

| Strategy | Example | Profiles helped |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| Text + image + diagram | Same concept shown as paragraph, illustration, and flowchart | NEE, visual learners |
| Highlighted vocabulary | Key terms bolded with contextual definition | NEE, ELL |
| Graphic organizers | Mind maps, tables, Venn diagrams | NEE, analytical learners |
| Audio alternative | Teacher reads aloud or audio recording available | NEE (dyslexia), auditory learners |

### Principle 2: Multiple Means of Engagement (the WHY)

Sustain motivation and effort through relevance, choice, and self-regulation.

| Strategy | Example | Profiles helped |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| Real-world relevance | Connect math to Ecuadorian market prices | Low motivation |
| Choice of activity | Select between writing, drawing, or presenting | All profiles |
| Cooperative options | Pair work, small group, whole class options | Social learners, NEE |
| Metacognitive prompts | "Mi emocion cuenta" — how does this topic make you feel? | Self-regulation |

### Principle 3: Multiple Means of Action & Expression (the HOW)

Allow diverse ways to demonstrate learning.

| Strategy | Example | Profiles helped |
|----------|---------|----------------|
| Multimodal response | Accept oral, written, visual, or project-based responses | NEE (dyslexia, motor) |
| Scaffolded steps | Break complex task into 3-4 numbered steps | NEE, low executive function |
| Technology option | QR-linked digital activity as alternative | Digital natives, NEE |
| Template/organizer | Provide sentence starters or graphic template | NEE, struggling writers |

## Rubric Design Patterns

### Structure: 3+ Performance Levels

| Level | Label | Score Range | Description Pattern |
|-------|-------|-------------|-------------------|
| Basico | "En desarrollo" | 1-2 | Student attempts but shows partial or incorrect performance |
| Intermedio | "Logrado" | 3-4 | Student demonstrates competency with minor gaps |
| Avanzado | "Destacado" | 5 | Student exceeds expectations with autonomy and depth |

### Observable Descriptor Rules

- Use action verbs (identifica, clasifica, produce, compara)
- Include quantity thresholds ("3 de 4 elementos", "al menos 2 ejemplos")
- Avoid subjective terms ("good", "adequate", "sufficient")
- Do not penalize expression modality (DUA Principle 3)

### Example Rubric Row

| Criterio | Basico (1-2) | Intermedio (3-4) | Avanzado (5) |
|----------|-------------|------------------|--------------|
| Identifica elementos narrativos | Identifica 1 de 4 elementos (personaje, escenario, trama, conflicto) | Identifica 2-3 de 4 elementos con descripcion parcial | Identifica los 4 elementos y explica su funcion en la historia |

## Weekly Planning Template

### Standard Distribution (EGB Media)

| Day | Session Length | Typical Activities |
|-----|--------------|-------------------|
| Lunes | 45 min | Apertura + Experiencia (new theme) |
| Martes | 45 min | Conceptualizacion |
| Miercoles | 45 min | Reflexion + Aplicacion |
| Jueves | 45 min | Next theme or deepening |
| Viernes | 45 min | Cierre + Evaluacion formativa |

### Time Allocation Guidelines

| Activity Type | Suggested Time | Rationale |
|--------------|---------------|-----------|
| Teacher exposition | Max 15 min | Attention span at 10-12 years is 15-20 min |
| Individual practice | 10-15 min | Sufficient for focused task completion |
| Group work | 15-20 min | Allows collaboration + sharing |
| Metacognitive wrap-up | 5-10 min | "What did we learn?" closure |

## Indicator Coverage Verification

For each indicador de logro from the unit's destrezas:

1. Verify a rubric exists with 3+ levels
2. Verify at least one formative activity assesses it
3. Verify at least one summative item assesses it
4. Verify the rubric descriptors use observable verbs

Coverage < 100% is a blocking issue for Gate G1.
