# State of the Art — Risk Monitoring (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: AI-Powered Risk Prediction

ML models trained on historical project data predict risk materialization 3-6 sprints ahead. Automated scanning of project artifacts (commits, PRs, Slack) detects risk signals human monitors miss. [DOC]

> "Principio Rector: Monitorear riesgos es anticipar el futuro con datos del presente."

## Trend 2: Real-Time Risk Dashboards

Static risk registers replaced by dynamic dashboards that aggregate risk data from multiple sources (Jira, Git, CI/CD, finance systems) and update continuously. [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 3: Risk Signal Integration

Risk monitoring systems consume signals from diverse sources — code quality metrics, team sentiment analysis, market indicators, vendor performance data — to create holistic risk pictures. [DOC]

## Trend 4: Automated Risk Response Triggering

When KRI thresholds are breached, automated responses activate: budget holds, stakeholder notifications, resource reallocation requests, or quality gate enforcement. [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 5: Risk Monitoring as Observability

Borrowing from DevOps observability (metrics, logs, traces), risk monitoring instruments projects with risk metrics, risk event logs, and risk causal traces for real-time observability. [DOC]

| DevOps Concept | Risk Monitoring Analog |
|---------------|----------------------|
| Metrics | Key Risk Indicators (KRIs) |
| Logs | Risk event audit trail |
| Traces | Risk causal chain analysis |
| Alerts | Threshold breach notifications |
| Dashboards | Risk heat maps and trend views |
