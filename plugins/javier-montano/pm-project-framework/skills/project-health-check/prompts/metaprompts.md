# Metaprompts — Project Health Check

## Meta-Strategy 1: Optimism Bias Correction

**Purpose:** Counter the natural tendency to report Green when Amber is warranted

```
For each health dimension rated Green, apply these challenges:
1. WATERMELON TEST — Is it Green on the outside but Red on the inside? Check lagging indicators.
2. SURVIVORSHIP BIAS — Are you only seeing the successful metrics and ignoring silent failures?
3. TREND BLINDNESS — Is the metric Green today but declining? A Green with downward trend is Amber.
4. COMPARISON BIAS — Green relative to plan, but is the plan itself realistic?
5. RECENCY BIAS — Did one good sprint mask a pattern of underperformance?
Reclassify any dimension that fails 2+ challenges.
```

## Meta-Strategy 2: Multi-Stakeholder Perspective Validation

**Purpose:** Ensure health assessment reflects diverse viewpoints

```
Validate the health check from 4 perspectives:
SPONSOR VIEW: Does the project still deliver strategic value on acceptable timeline?
PM VIEW: Are execution metrics within tolerance and team performing?
TEAM VIEW: Is workload sustainable, are blockers being removed, is quality achievable?
CUSTOMER VIEW: Are deliverables meeting expectations, is communication adequate?
For each perspective, identify the #1 concern and verify it is addressed in the health report.
```

## Meta-Strategy 3: Leading vs. Lagging Indicator Balance

**Purpose:** Ensure health checks are predictive, not just retrospective

```
Audit the health check for indicator balance:
LAGGING (what happened): SPI, CPI, defect count, milestone completion
LEADING (what will happen): Backlog readiness, team capacity, risk trajectory, stakeholder engagement trend
For each health dimension:
- Verify at least one leading indicator is included
- If only lagging indicators exist, identify and add a leading indicator
- Weight leading indicators at 60% for forward-looking health score
```
