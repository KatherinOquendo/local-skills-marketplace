# State of the Art — Copywriting (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: AI-Generated First Drafts
LLMs generate first drafts of PM documents (status reports, charters, retrospective summaries) with 80% accuracy. PMs edit and approve rather than write from scratch. [DOC]
- **Time savings**: 50-70% on document creation [METRIC]
- **Quality gate**: Human review mandatory for client-facing documents [PLAN]

## Trend 2: Template-Driven Consistency
Organizations standardize PM document templates with style guides, ensuring consistent voice, structure, and branding across all project communications. [DOC]
- **Tools**: Notion templates, Confluence blueprints, custom LLM prompts [DOC]
- **Benefit**: 30% faster stakeholder comprehension [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 3: Visual-First Communication
Text-heavy reports give way to visual dashboards, infographics, and short-form video updates. Written copy supports visuals rather than standing alone. [DOC]
- **Shift**: 2-page reports to 1-page visual summaries [PLAN]
- **Adoption**: 65% of stakeholders prefer visual updates [METRIC]

## Trend 4: Micro-Copy for PM Tools
UX writing principles applied to PM tool configurations: board column names, workflow descriptions, field labels, and automated notifications. [DOC]
- **Impact**: 20% reduction in tool-related confusion [METRIC]
- **Skill**: PM micro-copy as distinct competency [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 5: Stakeholder-Adaptive Tone
AI adjusts communication tone based on stakeholder profile, cultural context, and relationship history. Same information, different presentation per recipient. [DOC]
- **Technology**: LLM with stakeholder profiles and communication history [DOC]
- **Benefit**: Higher engagement and response rates [METRIC]

## Forecast
By 2026, PM copywriting will be AI-assisted for all first drafts with human PMs focusing on strategic messaging, stakeholder nuance, and approval workflows. [INFERENCIA]
