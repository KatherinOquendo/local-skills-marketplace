# Body of Knowledge — Funding Request

## 1. Foundations of Project Funding

Funding requests represent the formal mechanism through which project managers secure financial resources for initiatives. The discipline draws from capital budgeting theory, organizational finance, and strategic portfolio management. [DOC]

### 1.1 Capital Budgeting Framework

| Technique | Application | PM Relevance |
|-----------|-------------|--------------|
| Net Present Value (NPV) | Discounted cash flow analysis | Investment justification |
| Internal Rate of Return (IRR) | Breakeven rate identification | Comparative project ranking |
| Payback Period | Time to recover investment | Risk-averse stakeholders |
| Benefit-Cost Ratio (BCR) | Value per unit invested | Portfolio prioritization |
| Real Options Analysis | Staged investment flexibility | Agile/phased funding |

### 1.2 Funding Models in Project Management

1. **Lump-Sum Allocation** — Full budget approved upfront; common in waterfall. [DOC]
2. **Incremental Funding** — Stage-gate releases tied to milestones. [PLAN]
3. **Rolling Wave Funding** — Near-term detailed, far-term estimated. [INFERENCIA]
4. **Zero-Based Budgeting** — Every cycle starts from zero justification. [DOC]
5. **Venture-Style Funding** — Internal VC model for innovation projects. [INFERENCIA]

## 2. Standards and Frameworks

### 2.1 PMI Standards (PMBOK 7th Edition)

The Performance Domain of "Planning" encompasses funding request preparation as part of cost management. The Standard for Project Management emphasizes value delivery over budget compliance. [DOC]

### 2.2 PRINCE2 Business Case

PRINCE2 mandates a living Business Case document that includes funding requirements, expected benefits, and investment appraisal. The Business Case is reviewed at every stage gate. [DOC]

### 2.3 SAFe Lean Budgets

The Scaled Agile Framework replaces project-level funding with value-stream budgets, using guardrails and participatory budgeting. [DOC]

## 4. Stakeholder Alignment

### 4.1 Decision-Making Bodies

- **Steering Committee** — Strategic alignment validation
- **Finance Committee** — Budget availability and compliance
- **PMO** — Portfolio fit and resource capacity
- **Sponsor** — Business case ownership

### 4.2 Influence Strategies

1. Anchor with data — lead with quantified impact [METRIC]
2. Frame as risk mitigation — cost of inaction analysis [INFERENCIA]
3. Demonstrate quick wins — phased delivery model [PLAN]
4. Social proof — reference similar successful initiatives [DOC]

## 5. Financial Analysis Techniques

### 5.1 Sensitivity Analysis

Testing how changes in key assumptions affect the funding need:
- Best case / most likely / worst case scenarios
- Monte Carlo simulation for confidence intervals
- Tornado diagrams for variable impact ranking

### 5.2 Earned Value Integration

Linking funding requests to EVM metrics for in-flight projects:
- EAC (Estimate at Completion) drives supplemental funding
- TCPI (To-Complete Performance Index) signals funding adequacy
- VAC (Variance at Completion) triggers re-baseline requests

## 6. Governance and Compliance

| Threshold | Approval Authority | Documentation Required |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------------|
| < 50K USD equiv. | Department Head | Brief + estimates |
| 50K-250K | PMO Director | Full business case |
| 250K-1M | Steering Committee | Business case + risk register |
| > 1M | Board / C-Suite | Full investment proposal |

## 7. Common Pitfalls

1. **Optimism Bias** — Underestimating costs by 20-50% on average [INFERENCIA]
2. **Scope Creep in Estimates** — Including unfunded scope expansions
3. **Ignoring Opportunity Cost** — Not comparing against alternative investments
4. **Poor Timing** — Submitting outside budget cycles
5. **Lack of Contingency** — No management reserve allocation
