# Body of Knowledge — Communication Plan

## 1. Foundations

A communication plan defines who needs what information, when, through which channels, and in what format. It ensures stakeholders receive timely, relevant project information aligned with their needs and influence levels. [DOC]

### 1.1 Communication Matrix

| Stakeholder Group | Information Need | Channel | Frequency | Format | Owner |
|-------------------|-----------------|---------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Sponsor | Strategic status, decisions needed | Email + meeting | Bi-weekly | Executive brief [STAKEHOLDER] |
| Steering Committee | Governance, gate reviews | Formal meeting | Monthly | Presentation [STAKEHOLDER] |
| Team | Sprint details, blockers | Slack + standup | Daily | Informal [SCHEDULE] |
| Client | Progress, demos | Video call | Per sprint | Demo + report [STAKEHOLDER] |
| PMO | Portfolio metrics | Dashboard | Weekly | Automated [METRIC] |

### 1.2 Communication Types

| Type | Purpose | Examples |
|------|---------|---------|
| Push | Proactive information delivery | Status reports, newsletters [DOC] |
| Pull | Information available on demand | Dashboards, wikis [METRIC] |
| Interactive | Two-way exchange | Meetings, workshops [STAKEHOLDER] |
| Escalation | Issue/risk notification | Alert emails, war room [SCHEDULE] |

## 3. Communication Channels Effectiveness

| Channel | Richness | Speed | Documentation | Best For |
|---------|----------|-------|--------------|----------|
| Face-to-face/Video | Highest | Medium | Low | Complex topics, conflict [STAKEHOLDER] |
| Slack/Teams | Medium | Highest | Medium | Quick updates, Q&A [SCHEDULE] |
| Email | Medium | Medium | High | Formal communications [DOC] |
| Dashboard | Low | Real-time | High | Metrics, status [METRIC] |
| Wiki/Confluence | Low | Low | Highest | Documentation, knowledge [DOC] |
