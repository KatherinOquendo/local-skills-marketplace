# Metaprompts — Capacity Planning

## Meta-Strategy 1: Hidden Capacity Drains

**Purpose**: Identify capacity leaks not visible in formal planning.

**Strategy**:
Audit for hidden capacity consumers:
1. Untracked meetings and ad-hoc requests
2. Technical debt maintenance not in backlog
3. Support/on-call rotations
4. Knowledge sharing and mentoring time
5. Tool and environment maintenance

**Application template**:
> For `{team}`, conduct a capacity drain audit. Survey team on time spent on unplanned/untracked activities. Typical finding: 15-25% of capacity is consumed by invisible work. Adjust capacity model to account for discovered drains. [METRIC]

## Meta-Strategy 2: Sustainable Pace Validation

**Purpose**: Ensure capacity plans do not lead to burnout.

**Strategy**:
Validate that planned utilization stays within sustainable bounds:
- Target: 70-80% utilization for creative/complex work
- Warning: >85% utilization for more than 2 consecutive sprints
- Critical: >90% utilization signals burnout risk

**Application template**:
> Check planned utilization for `{team}` over next `{period}`. If any individual exceeds 85% for 2+ sprints, recommend workload redistribution. Track overtime trends as leading indicator. Sustainable pace is a project risk factor, not a personal matter. [STAKEHOLDER]

## Meta-Strategy 3: Capacity Scenario Planning

**Purpose**: Prepare for capacity disruptions.

**Strategy**:
Model 3 scenarios for capacity planning:
- **Baseline**: Current team, no changes
- **Pessimistic**: 1-2 team members leave, hiring delayed
- **Optimistic**: Hiring completes early, velocity improves

**Application template**:
> For `{project}`, model all three capacity scenarios over next quarter. For pessimistic scenario, identify which backlog items would be deferred. Present impact on milestones and commitments. Ensure sponsor understands the risk range. [PLAN]
