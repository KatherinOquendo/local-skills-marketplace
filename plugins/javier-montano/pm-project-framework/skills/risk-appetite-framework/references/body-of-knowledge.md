# Body of Knowledge — Risk Appetite Framework

## 1. Definition and Scope

A risk appetite framework defines the level and types of risk an organization is willing to accept in pursuit of its strategic objectives. It establishes boundaries for risk-taking, guides decision-making, and aligns project risk management with organizational risk tolerance. [DOC]

## 3. Key Concepts

| Concept | Definition | Example |
|---------|-----------|---------|
| Risk Appetite | Amount of risk willing to accept overall | "We accept moderate technology risk" |
| Risk Tolerance | Acceptable variation around objectives | "Schedule variance ±15% acceptable" |
| Risk Threshold | Specific trigger points for action | "If CPI < 0.85, escalate immediately" |
| Risk Capacity | Maximum risk able to absorb | "Can absorb 20% budget overrun maximum" |

## 4. Risk Appetite Dimensions

### 4.1 Strategic Risk Appetite
- Innovation risk acceptance level
- Market expansion risk tolerance
- Strategic partnership risk thresholds [PLAN]

### 4.2 Operational Risk Appetite
- Process failure tolerance levels
- Technology disruption acceptance
- Supply chain risk thresholds [METRIC]

### 4.3 Financial Risk Appetite
- Budget overrun tolerance (FTE-months)
- Investment loss acceptance
- Cash flow variability thresholds [METRIC]

### 4.4 Compliance Risk Appetite
- Regulatory deviation tolerance (typically zero)
- Audit finding acceptance levels
- Legal exposure thresholds [PLAN]

## 5. Risk Appetite Statement Structure

1. **Context** — Strategic objectives the appetite supports
2. **Appetite levels** — Per risk category (low/moderate/high)
3. **Tolerance ranges** — Quantified acceptable variation
4. **Thresholds** — Specific trigger points for escalation
5. **Governance** — Who sets, reviews, and updates appetite
6. **Review cadence** — How often appetite is reassessed [PLAN]

## 6. Risk Appetite Scale

| Level | Description | Acceptable Probability | Acceptable Impact |
|-------|-------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Averse | Minimal risk, certainty preferred | < 5% | Negligible |
| Cautious | Preference for safe options | < 15% | Minor |
| Moderate | Balanced risk-return | < 30% | Moderate |
| Open | Willing to consider higher risk | < 50% | Significant |
| Hungry | Aggressive pursuit of opportunity | > 50% | Major |
