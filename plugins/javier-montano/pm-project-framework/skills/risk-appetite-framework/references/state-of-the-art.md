# State of the Art — Risk Appetite Framework (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: Dynamic Risk Appetite

Static annual risk appetite statements replaced by dynamic appetites that adjust based on market conditions, portfolio health, and organizational capacity. AI monitors external signals and recommends appetite adjustments. [DOC]

> "Principio Rector: El apetito de riesgo es un acto estratégico, no un formulario de cumplimiento."

## Trend 2: Project-Level Appetite Cascading

Organizations cascading enterprise risk appetite into project-specific thresholds. Each project inherits organizational appetite but can negotiate tighter or looser bounds based on strategic importance. [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 3: Quantified Risk Appetite

Moving from qualitative statements ("moderate risk tolerance") to quantified thresholds ("accept up to 15% schedule variance, 10% effort overrun, and 5% scope reduction"). [DOC]

## Trend 4: Risk Appetite in Agile Contexts

Agile organizations expressing risk appetite through sprint-level constraints: acceptable defect escape rate, maximum WIP, minimum test coverage — operational thresholds that embody risk appetite. [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 5: ESG Risk Appetite Integration

Risk appetite frameworks now include explicit ESG risk dimensions, with organizations defining tolerance for environmental, social, and governance risks alongside traditional categories. [DOC]

| Risk Category | Traditional Appetite | 2025+ Appetite |
|--------------|---------------------|---------------|
| Financial | Moderate | Moderate |
| Operational | Cautious | Cautious |
| Strategic | Open | Open |
| Compliance | Averse | Averse |
