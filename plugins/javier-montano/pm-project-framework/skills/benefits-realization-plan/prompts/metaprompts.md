# Metaprompts — Benefits Realization Plan

## Meta-Strategy 1: Optimism Bias Correction

**Purpose**: Counter the systematic overestimation of benefits in business cases.

**Strategy**:
Apply reference class forecasting:
1. Find similar past projects in the organization
2. Compare claimed vs. realized benefits
3. Apply historical realization rate as discount factor
4. Present both optimistic and adjusted projections

**Application template**:
> For `{project}` benefits estimates, apply optimism bias correction. Find 3-5 comparable past projects. Calculate average realization rate. Apply to current estimates. If no comparables, use 60% industry benchmark. [INFERENCIA]

## Meta-Strategy 2: Benefits Attribution Rigor

**Purpose**: Ensure benefits can be credibly attributed to the project.

**Strategy**:
Classify each benefit by attribution strength:
- **Direct**: Entirely caused by project deliverable
- **Contributing**: One of several factors
- **Indirect**: Project enables conditions for benefit

**Application template**:
> Classify each benefit in `{register}` by attribution strength. For contributing and indirect benefits, estimate project contribution percentage. Flag benefits with <30% attribution confidence. [STAKEHOLDER]

## Meta-Strategy 3: Dis-Benefits Identification

**Purpose**: Systematically identify negative outcomes alongside positive benefits.

**Strategy**:
For every claimed benefit, ask: "What could go wrong? What might get worse?" Common dis-benefits: increased complexity, temporary productivity loss, change fatigue.

**Application template**:
> For each benefit, identify 1-2 potential dis-benefits. Estimate magnitude relative to benefit. If dis-benefit >30% of benefit value, flag for mitigation. Include in tracking dashboard. [PLAN]
