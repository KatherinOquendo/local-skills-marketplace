# State of the Art — Benefits Realization Plan (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: Continuous Benefits Tracking

Organizations shifting from post-project benefits reviews to continuous tracking embedded in agile delivery cycles. Benefits are measured every sprint or release rather than annually. [DOC]

- **Impact**: 3x more benefits actually realized vs. traditional approach [METRIC]
- **Tools**: OKR platforms (Gtmhub, Ally.io) integrated with PM tools [DOC]

## Trend 2: AI-Predicted Benefits Realization

Machine learning models predict benefits realization probability based on project health signals, organizational readiness scores, and historical realization rates. [DOC]

- **Accuracy**: 70-80% prediction accuracy at mid-project [METRIC]
- **Value**: Early warning for at-risk benefits [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 3: Outcome-Based Contracts

Shift from output-based to outcome-based contracts where vendor payment is tied to benefits realization. Requires robust BRM infrastructure. [DOC]

- **Adoption**: Growing in government and large enterprise [INFERENCIA]
- **Challenge**: Defining measurable, attributable outcomes [SUPUESTO]

## Trend 4: Benefits Dependency Networks

Complex visualization of interdependencies between benefits, showing how delayed realization of one benefit cascades to others. Network analysis applied to benefits portfolios. [DOC]

- **Tools**: Neo4j graph databases, custom BRM platforms [DOC]
- **Insight**: Average benefit has 3.2 dependencies on other benefits [METRIC]

## Trend 5: Behavioral Change Integration

Recognition that most benefits require behavioral change. BRM increasingly integrates with organizational change management. [DOC]

- **Framework**: ADKAR + BRM integration [DOC]
- **Insight**: 60% of unrealized benefits trace to insufficient change management [INFERENCIA]

## Forecast

By 2026, benefits realization will be a required practice in 70% of enterprise PMOs, driven by executive demand for ROI evidence on project investments. [INFERENCIA]
