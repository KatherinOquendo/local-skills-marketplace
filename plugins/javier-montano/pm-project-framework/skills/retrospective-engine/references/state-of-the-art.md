# State of the Art — Retrospective Engine (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: Data-Driven Retrospectives

Retrospectives now start with automated data collection — velocity charts, cycle time distributions, defect trends, and deployment frequency — providing objective context before subjective discussion. [DOC]

> "Principio Rector: Reflexionar con datos es transformar opiniones en mejora sistémica."

## Trend 2: AI-Facilitated Retrospectives

AI assistants analyze sprint data, suggest discussion topics based on anomalies, and generate improvement hypotheses. AI can identify patterns humans miss (e.g., correlation between PR review delays and late-sprint defects). [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 3: Async Retrospectives for Distributed Teams

Remote and distributed teams adopting async retro formats — participants contribute observations asynchronously via tools like Miro, FunRetro, or custom boards, then synchronize for a focused 30-minute discussion. [DOC]

## Trend 4: Improvement Experiments with Metrics

Actions from retros framed as experiments with hypotheses, metrics, and time-boxes. "We believe that limiting WIP to 3 per developer will reduce cycle time by 20% within 2 sprints." [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 5: Cross-Team and Program Retrospectives

Scaled retrospectives where improvement themes from team retros are aggregated at program level, identifying systemic issues that require organizational intervention. [DOC]

| Level | Scope | Frequency | Focus |
|-------|-------|-----------|-------|
| Team | Single team sprint | Every sprint | Tactical improvements |
| Program | Multiple teams | Quarterly | Cross-team patterns |
| Organization | Portfolio-wide | Semi-annual | Systemic improvements |
