# State of the Art — Hybrid Assessment (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: AI-Driven Methodology Recommendation

Machine learning models analyze project characteristics and recommend optimal hybrid configurations based on historical success patterns across thousands of projects. [INFERENCIA]

- **Evidence**: PMI and Gartner research on predictive methodology selection (2024-2025)
- **Impact**: Data-driven methodology decisions; risk of over-fitting to past patterns
- **Maturity**: Early Adopter

## Trend 2: Continuous Methodology Calibration

Rather than a one-time assessment, organizations implement continuous monitoring of methodology effectiveness with quarterly recalibration cycles. [DOC]

- **Evidence**: Disciplined Agile's "Choose Your WoW" promotes ongoing process improvement
- **Impact**: Better fit over time; requires measurement infrastructure
- **Maturity**: Early Majority

## Trend 3: Flow-Based Assessment

Assessment shifting from methodology labels to flow metrics (cycle time, throughput, WIP). The focus is on delivery flow rather than whether the approach is "agile" or "waterfall." [DOC]

- **Evidence**: Flow Framework (Mik Kersten) and Kanban Maturity Model adoption
- **Impact**: Outcome-focused assessment; requires tooling for flow metrics
- **Maturity**: Early Adopter to Early Majority

## Trend 4: Context-Aware Governance Templates

Governance models auto-adapt based on assessment results. A project assessed as "high agile fit" automatically receives lightweight governance; "high predictive fit" gets traditional gates. [INFERENCIA]

- **Evidence**: ServiceNow and Planview implementing adaptive governance workflows
- **Impact**: Reduced governance overhead; requires well-defined assessment criteria
- **Maturity**: Early Adopter
