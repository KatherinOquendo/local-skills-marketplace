# State of the Art — Strategic Alignment (2024-2026)

> Current trends, emerging practices, and open debates in strategy-to-project alignment.

---

## 1. Dominant Trends

### 1.1 Continuous Strategy Alignment (Not Annual)

- **What's happening:** Organizations are moving from annual strategic planning cycles to continuous strategy sensing and adaptation. Quarterly OKR reviews replace annual strategic plans, requiring more frequent project alignment checks.
- **Impact on skill:** Alignment analysis should be refreshed quarterly at minimum, not annually. Build quarterly alignment review into governance cadence.
- **Evidence:** McKinsey (2025). *Strategy in a Turbulent World: Continuous Adaptation*.

### 1.2 AI-Powered Strategy Mapping

- **What's happening:** LLMs analyze project portfolios, strategic documents, and OKRs to automatically suggest alignment mappings and identify orphans/gaps. This reduces manual mapping effort for large portfolios.
- **Impact on skill:** AI can pre-populate alignment matrices. Tag AI-suggested mappings [INFERENCIA] until validated by portfolio managers.
- **Evidence:** Gartner (2025). *AI in Strategic Portfolio Management*.

### 1.3 Outcome-Based Portfolio Management

- **What's happening:** Shift from project-based to outcome-based portfolio management. Instead of tracking project completion, track Key Result achievement. A completed project that doesn't move the KR metric is a failed investment.
- **Impact on skill:** Alignment scoring should weight outcome contribution higher than output delivery. Include KR progress tracking in alignment assessment.
- **Evidence:** PMI (2024). *Benefits Realization Management Practice Guide — 2nd Edition*.

### 1.4 Strategy Execution Platforms

- **What's happening:** Tools (Perdoo, Quantive, Gtmhub) connect strategic objectives to project portfolios with automated alignment tracking, real-time KR progress, and alignment drift alerts.
- **Impact on skill:** Recommend strategy execution platforms for organizations with >20 active projects. Manual alignment tracking scales poorly.
- **Evidence:** Forrester (2025). *Strategy Execution Management Wave*.

---

## 2. Open Debates

### 2.1 OKRs vs. Balanced Scorecard: Which Is Better?
- OKRs are simpler and more agile; Balanced Scorecard is more comprehensive. Emerging consensus: OKRs for execution, Balanced Scorecard for holistic strategy health checks.

### 2.2 Should Every Project Trace to Strategy?
- Purists say yes. Pragmatists note that operational maintenance, technical debt, and compliance projects may not trace to strategic objectives but are essential. The compromise: distinguish "strategic" projects from "operational" projects with different governance expectations.
