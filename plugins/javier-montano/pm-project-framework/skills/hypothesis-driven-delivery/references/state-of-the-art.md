# State of the Art — Hypothesis-Driven Delivery (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: AI-Powered Experiment Design

AI tools automatically generate hypothesis statements, suggest experiment designs, calculate sample sizes, and predict outcomes based on historical experiment data. [INFERENCIA]

- **Evidence**: Experimentation platforms (Optimizely, LaunchDarkly) adding AI-assisted hypothesis generation
- **Impact**: Faster experiment cycles; risk of automated confirmation bias
- **Maturity**: Early Adopter

## Trend 2: Continuous Experimentation Platforms

Feature flagging and experimentation merge into continuous delivery pipelines. Every deployment is a potential experiment with built-in measurement. [DOC]

- **Evidence**: LaunchDarkly, Split.io, and Statsig report 300% growth in experiment volume (2024)
- **Impact**: Democratized experimentation; requires data literacy across teams
- **Maturity**: Early Majority

## Trend 3: HDD in Non-Software Domains

Hypothesis-driven approaches expanding beyond software into organizational change, process improvement, and strategic planning. Business experiments replace traditional business cases. [INFERENCIA]

- **Evidence**: McKinsey "Test and Learn" methodology adopted by Fortune 500 for strategy
- **Impact**: Broader applicability; cultural shift required
- **Maturity**: Early Adopter

## Trend 4: Experiment Portfolios

Organizations manage experiments as portfolios with investment allocation, kill criteria, and option-value analysis. Portfolio theory applied to innovation bets. [DOC]

- **Evidence**: Lean Portfolio Management in SAFe 6.0; innovation accounting practices
- **Impact**: Better resource allocation to experiments; complexity in tracking
- **Maturity**: Early Adopter
