# Financial Business Case — Body of Knowledge

## Standards & Frameworks
- **PMBOK 7th Edition**: Business case as project justification artifact
- **PRINCE2**: Business case as living document throughout project lifecycle
- **ISO 21502:2020**: Investment appraisal and benefits management

## Business Case Components
| Component | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| Executive Summary | 1-page justification for decision-makers |
| Problem/Opportunity | Quantified business need |
| Options Analysis | ≥3 options including "do nothing" |
| Cost-Benefit Analysis | NPV, IRR, payback period, ROI |
| Risk Assessment | Financial risks with probability-weighted impact |
| Implementation Timeline | High-level milestones with cost phasing |
| Benefits Realization | Measurable benefits with tracking plan |

## Financial Analysis Methods
- **NPV (Net Present Value)**: Sum of discounted cash flows. NPV > 0 = viable
- **IRR (Internal Rate of Return)**: Discount rate where NPV = 0. IRR > hurdle rate = viable
- **Payback Period**: Time to recover investment. Shorter = lower risk
- **ROI**: (Net Benefits / Total Cost) × 100. Higher = better return
- **BCR (Benefit-Cost Ratio)**: Total Benefits / Total Costs. BCR > 1 = viable

## Options Analysis Framework
1. **Do Nothing** — Baseline for comparison, quantify cost of inaction
2. **Do Minimum** — Least investment to address core problem
3. **Recommended Option** — Optimal balance of cost, benefit, and risk
4. **Do Maximum** — Full scope, highest investment, maximum benefit potential

## Sensitivity Analysis
Test key assumptions: ±10%, ±20%, ±30% variation on:
- Cost estimates (labor, infrastructure, licensing)
- Benefit projections (revenue, savings, productivity)
- Timeline (delays increase costs, defer benefits)
- Discount rate (organizational cost of capital)
