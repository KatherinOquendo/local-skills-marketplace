# State of the Art — Schedule Baseline (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: Probabilistic Scheduling as Default

Deterministic single-point schedules replaced by probabilistic ranges. Teams present P50/P80/P90 completion dates rather than single target dates, improving stakeholder trust. [DOC]

> "Principio Rector: Un cronograma honesto es probabilístico, no determinístico."

## Trend 2: AI-Assisted Schedule Generation

AI tools generate initial schedules from WBS and historical data, identify optimal sequencing, and predict duration estimates based on team velocity and complexity patterns. [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 3: Continuous Re-Baselining in Agile

Agile projects use rolling-wave baselines that re-baseline at PI boundaries rather than maintaining a single fixed baseline. Near-term detail, far-term milestones. [DOC]

## Trend 4: Flow-Based Scheduling

Kanban-influenced scheduling using cumulative flow diagrams and Monte Carlo simulations based on throughput data rather than traditional CPM-based scheduling. [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 5: Integrated Schedule-Risk Dashboards

Schedule dashboards now integrate risk data, showing not just planned vs. actual but also risk-adjusted forecasts and confidence intervals for upcoming milestones. [DOC]

| View | Traditional | Modern |
|------|-------------|--------|
| Timeline | Gantt chart (single line) | Gantt with confidence bands |
| Progress | % complete | SPI + forecast |
| Risk | Separate risk register | Integrated risk overlay |
| Dependencies | Static links | Dynamic dependency health |
