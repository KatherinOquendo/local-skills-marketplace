# Body of Knowledge — Contingency Planning

## 1. Foundations
Contingency planning prepares alternative courses of action for identified risks, ensuring the project can respond effectively when risk events materialize. [DOC]

### 1.1 Contingency Types
| Type | Trigger | Scope | Authority |
|------|---------|-------|-----------|
| Schedule contingency | Milestone at risk | Timeline buffer | PM [SCHEDULE] |
| Budget contingency | Cost overrun threshold | Reserve draw | PM + Sponsor [PLAN] |
| Scope contingency | Requirement change | Pre-defined scope trade-offs | CCB [STAKEHOLDER] |
| Resource contingency | Key person unavailable | Backup resource plan | PM [PLAN] |
| Technical contingency | Technology failure | Alternative approach | Tech Lead [METRIC] |

### 1.2 Contingency Reserve Calculation
| Method | Formula | Best For |
|--------|---------|----------|
| Expected Monetary Value | Sum(Probability x Impact) | Quantified risks [METRIC] |
| Percentage of budget | 10-20% of BAC | Early-stage estimates [PLAN] |
| Monte Carlo simulation | P80 - P50 value | Complex projects [METRIC] |
| Expert judgment | Facilitated estimation | Novel projects [INFERENCIA] |

## 2. Contingency Plan Structure
1. **Risk trigger** — Specific event or threshold that activates the plan [METRIC]
2. **Response actions** — Step-by-step actions to execute [PLAN]
3. **Resource requirements** — People, budget, tools needed [SCHEDULE]
4. **Decision authority** — Who can activate the plan [STAKEHOLDER]
5. **Communication protocol** — Who to notify and how [STAKEHOLDER]
6. **Success criteria** — How to know the contingency worked [METRIC]
