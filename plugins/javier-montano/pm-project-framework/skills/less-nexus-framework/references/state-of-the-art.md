# State of the Art — LeSS/Nexus Framework (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: LeSS/Nexus as SAFe Alternative

Organizations seeking lighter scaling frameworks migrate from SAFe to LeSS or Nexus, reducing overhead while maintaining multi-team coordination. [INFERENCIA]

- **Evidence**: Scrum.org and LeSS Company report increased adoption by SAFe-fatigued enterprises (2024-2025)
- **Impact**: Simpler scaling; requires stronger engineering discipline
- **Maturity**: Early Adopter to Early Majority

## Trend 2: AI-Assisted Dependency Detection

AI tools analyze sprint backlogs across teams to automatically detect cross-team dependencies, reducing planning overhead for both LeSS and Nexus. [INFERENCIA]

- **Evidence**: Jira and Azure DevOps adding cross-team dependency analysis features
- **Impact**: Better dependency visibility; still requires human resolution
- **Maturity**: Early Adopter

## Trend 3: Remote-First Scaled Scrum

LeSS and Nexus adapted for distributed teams using digital collaboration tools (Miro, FigJam) for joint planning and integration events. [DOC]

- **Evidence**: Post-pandemic scaling patterns documented by LeSS Company and Scrum.org
- **Impact**: Geographical flexibility; coordination overhead increases
- **Maturity**: Early Majority

## Trend 4: Feature Team Adoption Acceleration

Organizations invest in feature team restructuring (from component teams) as a prerequisite for LeSS/Nexus, recognizing it as the highest-leverage scaling enabler. [DOC]

- **Evidence**: LeSS adoption guides emphasize team restructuring as Step 1
- **Impact**: Reduced inter-team dependencies; initial productivity dip during transition
- **Maturity**: Early Majority
