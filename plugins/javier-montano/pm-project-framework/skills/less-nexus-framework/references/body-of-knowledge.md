# Body of Knowledge — LeSS/Nexus Framework

## 1. Foundations of Scaled Scrum Frameworks

LeSS (Large-Scale Scrum) and Nexus are frameworks for scaling Scrum to multiple teams working on a single product. Both maintain Scrum's core principles while adding coordination mechanisms for 3-10+ teams. [DOC]

### 1.1 Framework Comparison

| Dimension | LeSS | Nexus |
|-----------|------|-------|
| Creator | Craig Larman & Bas Vodde | Ken Schwaber (Scrum.org) |
| Teams | 2-8 (LeSS), 8+ (LeSS Huge) | 3-9 teams |
| Product Owner | Single PO for all teams | Single PO for all teams |
| Integration | Continuous integration by all teams | Nexus Integration Team |
| Sprint Review | Joint review (all teams) | Joint Nexus Sprint Review |
| Backlog | Single Product Backlog | Single Product Backlog |
| Retrospective | Overall + team retros | Nexus + team retros |

### 1.2 Core Scaling Principles

1. **Single Product Backlog** — One backlog, one PO, one priority. [DOC]
2. **Cross-Functional Teams** — Each team can deliver end-to-end increment. [DOC]
3. **Continuous Integration** — All teams integrate every sprint. [DOC]
4. **Joint Sprint Review** — Single review for integrated increment. [DOC]
5. **Systems Thinking** — Optimize the whole, not individual teams. [DOC]

## 2. LeSS Framework Details

### 2.1 LeSS Events

| Event | Participants | Purpose |
|-------|-------------|---------|
| Sprint Planning Part 1 | All teams + PO | Select items, coordinate |
| Sprint Planning Part 2 | Individual teams | Design and task breakdown |
| Daily Scrum | Individual teams | Team synchronization |
| Overall Retrospective | Representatives + PO + SM | Cross-team improvement |
| Sprint Review | All teams + stakeholders | Inspect integrated increment |

### 2.2 LeSS Roles

| Role | Scope | Key Responsibility |
|------|-------|-------------------|
| Product Owner | Entire product | Prioritization, vision |
| Scrum Master | 1-3 teams | Process facilitation, coaching |
| Team Members | Cross-functional team | Delivery, integration |

### 2.3 LeSS Huge (8+ teams)

- Area Product Owners manage sub-backlogs
- Requirement Areas group related features
- Overall Product Owner maintains strategic alignment

## 3. Nexus Framework Details

### 3.1 Nexus Integration Team (NIT)

| Member | Role in NIT |
|--------|------------|
| Product Owner | Overall priority and integration vision |
| Scrum Master | Cross-team coordination facilitation |
| Integration Members | Technical integration specialists |

### 3.2 Nexus Events

| Event | Added Value vs Scrum |
|-------|---------------------|
| Nexus Sprint Planning | Cross-team dependency identification |
| Nexus Daily Scrum | Integration issue surfacing |
| Nexus Sprint Review | Integrated increment inspection |
| Nexus Sprint Retrospective | Cross-team process improvement |

## 4. Coordination Mechanisms

| Mechanism | LeSS | Nexus | Purpose |
|-----------|------|-------|---------|
| Scrum of Scrums | Optional | Via Nexus Daily Scrum | Daily coordination |
| Joint Planning | Sprint Planning Part 1 | Nexus Sprint Planning | Dependency management |
| Integration | Continuous | NIT facilitated | Product coherence |
| Communities of Practice | Recommended | Encouraged | Knowledge sharing |
| Multi-Team Refinement | Required | Recommended | Shared understanding |

## 5. When to Use Which

| Context | Recommended Framework | Rationale |
|---------|----------------------|-----------|
| Strong engineering practices | LeSS | Relies on team discipline |
| Need integration guidance | Nexus | NIT provides structure |
| 2-8 teams | LeSS | Native range |
| 3-9 teams | Nexus | Native range |
| 8+ teams | LeSS Huge | Area PO model scales |
| Regulatory environment | Nexus | More defined roles |

## 6. Common Pitfalls

1. **Feature Team Theater** — Component teams renamed but not restructured [INFERENCIA]
2. **Weak Product Owner** — PO cannot manage multiple teams' demand
3. **Integration Debt** — Deferring integration to end of sprint
4. **Too Many Teams** — Scaling before optimizing individual team performance
5. **Ignoring Dependencies** — Assuming cross-team coordination is automatic
