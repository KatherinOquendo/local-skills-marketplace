# Body of Knowledge — Cost Estimation

## 1. Foundations
Cost estimation determines the approximate resources needed to complete project activities. Accurate estimation is the foundation of budget baselines, business cases, and go/no-go decisions. [DOC]

### 1.1 Estimation Methods
| Method | Accuracy | Data Required | Best For |
|--------|----------|--------------|---------|
| Analogous | -25% to +75% | Similar past projects | Early phases [INFERENCIA] |
| Parametric | -15% to +25% | Historical data, parameters | Scalable components [METRIC] |
| Bottom-up | -5% to +10% | Detailed WBS | Execution planning [PLAN] |
| Three-point (PERT) | -10% to +15% | Expert judgment | Uncertainty modeling [METRIC] |
| Story point relative | Relative sizing | Team velocity history | Agile projects [METRIC] |

### 1.2 AACE Estimate Classification
| Class | Maturity | Expected Accuracy | Purpose |
|-------|----------|-------------------|---------|
| 5 | 0-2% definition | -20% to +100% | Concept screening [SUPUESTO] |
| 4 | 1-15% definition | -15% to +50% | Feasibility study [INFERENCIA] |
| 3 | 10-40% definition | -10% to +30% | Budget authorization [PLAN] |
| 2 | 30-75% definition | -5% to +20% | Control baseline [METRIC] |
| 1 | 65-100% definition | -3% to +15% | Definitive estimate [METRIC] |

## 2. Common Estimation Biases
1. **Optimism bias** — Systematically underestimating effort [INFERENCIA]
2. **Anchoring** — First estimate influences all subsequent estimates [INFERENCIA]
3. **Planning fallacy** — Ignoring base rates from similar projects [SUPUESTO]
4. **Scope creep blindness** — Estimating known scope, ignoring likely additions [PLAN]
