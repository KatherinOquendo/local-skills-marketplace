# State of the Art — Project Selection (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: AI-Driven Portfolio Optimization

Machine learning models analyze historical project outcomes to predict success probability for proposed initiatives. AI recommends optimal portfolio mixes that maximize strategic value while respecting resource constraints. [DOC]

> "Principio Rector: Seleccionar es el acto estratégico más poderoso del portafolio."

## Trend 2: Continuous Funding over Annual Budgeting

Lean Portfolio Management replaces annual project selection cycles with continuous funding decisions. Projects receive incremental funding based on demonstrated value, similar to venture capital staging. [INFERENCIA]

| Model | Cadence | Decision Style | Flexibility |
|-------|---------|---------------|-------------|
| Annual selection | Yearly | Big batch | Low |
| Quarterly review | 3 months | Medium batch | Medium |
| Continuous funding | On-demand | Incremental | High |

## Trend 3: Outcome-Based Selection

Selection criteria shifting from output specifications (build feature X) to outcome hypotheses (achieve metric Y). Projects compete on expected outcome impact rather than deliverable descriptions. [DOC]

## Trend 4: Real Options in Project Selection

Organizations applying real options theory — investing small amounts to create options for larger investments. Stage-gated selection with explicit pivot/persevere/kill decision points at each gate. [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 5: ESG and Social Impact Scoring

Project selection criteria now include mandatory ESG scoring alongside financial and strategic criteria. Impact-weighted accounting influences portfolio composition toward sustainable initiatives. [DOC]

| Selection Dimension | Traditional Weight | 2025+ Weight |
|--------------------|-------------------|-------------|
| Financial return | 40% | 30% |
| Strategic alignment | 30% | 25% |
| Risk profile | 20% | 15% |
| ESG / Social impact | 0% | 20% |
| Innovation potential | 10% | 10% |

## Emerging Practices

- **Portfolio simulation**: Monte Carlo simulation of portfolio outcomes under different selection scenarios
- **Crowdsourced scoring**: Broad organizational input on project value, reducing HiPPO bias
- **Dynamic prioritization**: Real-time reprioritization based on market signals and competitor moves
- **Kill criteria upfront**: Defining explicit conditions for project termination at selection time [INFERENCIA]
