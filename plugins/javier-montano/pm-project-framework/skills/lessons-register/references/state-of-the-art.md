# State of the Art — Lessons Register (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: AI-Powered Lesson Surfacing

NLP models automatically surface relevant lessons from the repository when new projects are initiated, matching on project type, technology, industry, and team composition. [DOC]

- **Evidence**: Knowledge management platforms (Guru, Notion AI, Confluence AI) adding semantic search
- **Impact**: Lessons actually get applied; requires quality input data
- **Maturity**: Early Majority

## Trend 2: Continuous Lesson Capture

Shift from periodic retrospectives to continuous micro-lessons captured in-flow via Slack bots, Jira comments, and voice notes automatically transcribed and categorized. [INFERENCIA]

- **Evidence**: Retrium, TeamRetro, and custom bots enabling continuous capture
- **Impact**: Higher volume of timely lessons; noise-to-signal ratio challenge
- **Maturity**: Early Adopter

## Trend 3: Cross-Organization Lesson Sharing

Anonymized lessons shared across organizations via industry consortiums and professional communities, expanding the learning base beyond company boundaries. [INFERENCIA]

- **Evidence**: PMI Wicked Problems initiative and Agile Alliance experience reports
- **Impact**: Broader learning pool; confidentiality challenges
- **Maturity**: Innovator to Early Adopter

## Trend 4: Lessons-Driven Process Automation

Validated lessons automatically trigger process template updates, checklist additions, and governance adjustments, closing the loop from learning to action. [DOC]

- **Evidence**: ServiceNow and Planview adding lesson-to-process automation workflows
- **Impact**: Structural learning; risk of premature standardization
- **Maturity**: Early Adopter
