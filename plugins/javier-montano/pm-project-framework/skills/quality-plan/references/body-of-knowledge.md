# Body of Knowledge — Quality Plan

## 1. Definition and Scope

A quality plan defines the quality standards, procedures, metrics, and responsibilities applicable to a specific project. It specifies how the organization's quality management system applies to the project context, ensuring deliverables meet stakeholder expectations and regulatory requirements. [DOC]

## 3. Quality Plan Components

### 3.1 Quality Objectives
- Measurable quality targets aligned to project objectives
- Acceptance criteria for each deliverable
- Customer satisfaction targets
- Process performance targets [METRIC]

### 3.2 Quality Standards
- Applicable industry standards (ISO, IEEE, OWASP)
- Organizational quality policies and procedures
- Regulatory and compliance requirements
- Customer-specific quality requirements [DOC]

### 3.3 Quality Roles and Responsibilities
- Quality manager/lead designation
- Team quality responsibilities
- Review and approval authorities
- Escalation paths for quality issues [STAKEHOLDER]

### 3.4 Quality Activities
- Reviews (peer, technical, management)
- Testing strategy and levels
- Inspections and walkthroughs
- Quality audits and assessments [PLAN]

### 3.5 Quality Metrics
- Defect density targets
- Test coverage requirements
- Review effectiveness rates
- Customer satisfaction scores [METRIC]

## 4. Quality Planning Process

| Step | Activity | Output |
|------|----------|--------|
| 1 | Identify quality requirements | Requirements traceability matrix |
| 2 | Define quality standards | Standards compliance matrix |
| 3 | Design quality activities | Quality activity schedule |
| 4 | Establish quality metrics | Metrics dashboard specification |
| 5 | Assign quality responsibilities | Quality RACI matrix |
| 6 | Define quality tools | Tool selection document |
| 7 | Plan quality reviews | Review calendar |

## 5. Cost of Quality

| Category | Examples | Relationship |
|----------|----------|-------------|
| Prevention costs | Training, process design, tools | Investment in quality |
| Appraisal costs | Testing, reviews, audits | Verification costs |
| Internal failure | Rework, defect fixes, retesting | Cost of poor quality |
| External failure | Customer complaints, warranty, recalls | Highest cost category |

## 6. Quality Tools and Techniques

- Cause-and-effect diagrams (Ishikawa/fishbone)
- Pareto analysis (80/20 rule for defect prioritization)
- Control charts (process stability monitoring)
- Checklists (standardized quality verification)
- Statistical sampling (inspection efficiency)
- Flowcharts (process visualization) [PLAN]
