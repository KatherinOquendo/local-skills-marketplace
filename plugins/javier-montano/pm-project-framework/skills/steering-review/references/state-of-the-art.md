# State of the Art — Steering Review (2024-2026)

> Current trends, emerging practices, and open debates in project governance and steering reviews.

---

## 1. Dominant Trends

### 1.1 AI-Augmented Gate Reviews

- **What's happening:** LLMs analyze project documentation packages and pre-generate advisor assessments, identifying gaps, inconsistencies, and risks across deliverables. Human advisors review and adjust AI-generated assessments rather than starting from scratch.
- **Impact on skill:** AI can pre-populate the evidence package analysis, flag missing deliverables, and identify cross-document inconsistencies. Human advisors focus on judgment and decision-making.
- **Evidence:** PMI (2025). *AI-Augmented Project Governance*; Gartner (2025). *AI in PPM Decision Support*.

### 1.2 Continuous Governance (Replacing Periodic Gates)

- **What's happening:** Agile organizations are replacing infrequent formal gates with continuous governance signals: automated quality metrics, risk dashboards, and exception-based escalation. Gates become "health checks" rather than "pass/fail exams."
- **Impact on skill:** Even with continuous monitoring, formal gate reviews remain valuable for investment decisions and organizational accountability. Continuous signals feed into gate evidence.
- **Evidence:** Scaled Agile (2024). *Lean Portfolio Management — Continuous Governance*.

### 1.3 Dissent-First Culture

- **What's happening:** High-performing governance bodies actively solicit dissent. Pre-mortem thinking applied to steering reviews: "assume this project will fail — what evidence supports that assumption?" This counteracts confirmation bias.
- **Impact on skill:** Include a mandatory "devil's advocate" phase in the review protocol where advisors are explicitly asked to argue against proceeding.
- **Evidence:** Kahneman, D. et al. (2024). *Noise and Bias in Organizational Decision-Making*.

### 1.4 Value-Based Gate Criteria

- **What's happening:** Traditional gate criteria focus on process compliance (did you produce the documents?). Modern criteria focus on value evidence (will this project deliver the expected value?). This shifts from checking boxes to validating assumptions.
- **Impact on skill:** Include value validation metrics in gate criteria alongside process compliance. "Is the business case still valid?" is more important than "Is the business case document complete?"
- **Evidence:** PMI (2024). *Benefits Realization Management Practice Guide — 2nd Edition*.

---

## 2. Emerging Patterns

### 2.1 Asynchronous Steering Reviews
- Advisors review evidence packages asynchronously, submit assessments and votes digitally, and synchronous meeting is reserved for discussing dissent and conditions only. Reduces meeting time from 2 hours to 30 minutes.

### 2.2 External Advisory Boards
- Organizations including external domain experts (industry consultants, technology partners) in steering reviews for independent perspective. External advisors bring market context internal advisors may lack.

---

## 3. Open Debates

### 3.1 How Many Gates Is Enough?
- Too few gates = insufficient governance oversight. Too many gates = bureaucratic overhead. The emerging consensus: gates proportional to project risk and investment size. Small projects: 2 gates (start, close). Large projects: 4-5 gates.

### 3.2 Should Steering Committees Have Veto Power?
- Some organizations give steering committees advisory-only authority (sponsor decides). Others give full decision authority. The debate: advisory authority requires strong sponsors; decision authority requires representative committees.
