# Body of Knowledge — Ceremony Design

## 1. Foundations

Ceremony design is the intentional crafting of recurring project events (standups, reviews, retros, planning) to maximize engagement, decision quality, and team alignment. [DOC]

### 1.1 Core Ceremonies by Framework

| Framework | Ceremony | Cadence | Duration | Purpose |
|-----------|---------|---------|----------|---------|
| Scrum | Sprint Planning | Per sprint | 2-4 hours | Commit to sprint scope [PLAN] |
| Scrum | Daily Standup | Daily | 15 min | Synchronize and surface blockers [SCHEDULE] |
| Scrum | Sprint Review | Per sprint | 1-2 hours | Inspect increment, get feedback [STAKEHOLDER] |
| Scrum | Retrospective | Per sprint | 1-1.5 hours | Continuous improvement [METRIC] |
| SAFe | PI Planning | Per PI (5 sprints) | 2 days | Cross-team alignment [PLAN] |
| Kanban | Replenishment | Weekly | 30 min | Prioritize incoming work [PLAN] |
| Kanban | Service Delivery Review | Bi-weekly | 1 hour | Flow metrics review [METRIC] |

### 1.2 Design Principles

1. **Purpose-driven** — Every ceremony has a clear, stated outcome [DOC]
2. **Time-boxed** — Strict time limits prevent ceremony fatigue [SCHEDULE]
3. **Inclusive** — All relevant voices heard, diverse facilitation techniques [STAKEHOLDER]
4. **Action-oriented** — Each ceremony produces decisions or action items [PLAN]
5. **Evolving** — Ceremony formats adapt based on team feedback [METRIC]

## 2. Ceremony Anti-Patterns

| Anti-Pattern | Symptom | Fix |
|-------------|---------|-----|
| Status meeting disguised as standup | Manager interrogates team members | Peer-to-peer format, walking the board |
| Retro without action | Good discussion, no follow-through | Limit to 3 actions with owners and due dates |
| Review without stakeholders | Only dev team attends | Stakeholder calendar blocks, compelling demos |
| Planning without preparation | Stories not refined, estimation chaos | Mandatory backlog refinement before planning |
| Ceremony overload | >25% of sprint in meetings | Audit and consolidate ceremonies |

## 3. Facilitation Techniques

| Technique | Best For | Engagement Level |
|-----------|---------|-----------------|
| Lean Coffee | Ad-hoc topic prioritization | High [STAKEHOLDER] |
| 1-2-4-All (Liberating Structures) | Large group ideation | High [STAKEHOLDER] |
| Dot Voting | Quick prioritization | Medium [METRIC] |
| Silent Writing | Introverted teams, bias reduction | High [INFERENCIA] |
| Round Robin | Equal voice distribution | Medium [STAKEHOLDER] |
| Fishbowl | Complex topic deep-dive | High [STAKEHOLDER] |

## 4. Ceremony Effectiveness Metrics

| Metric | Measurement | Target |
|--------|------------|--------|
| Attendance rate | Actual / expected participants | >90% [METRIC] |
| Action completion rate | Completed / generated actions | >80% [METRIC] |
| NPS (per ceremony) | Participant satisfaction survey | >7/10 [STAKEHOLDER] |
| Decision velocity | Decisions made per ceremony hour | Track trend [METRIC] |
| Time in ceremonies | % of sprint capacity | <20% [SCHEDULE] |
