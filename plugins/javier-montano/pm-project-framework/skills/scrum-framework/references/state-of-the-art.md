# State of the Art — Scrum Framework (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: AI-Augmented Scrum Events

AI tools assist sprint planning (story sizing, capacity analysis), daily scrums (automated blockers detection), and retrospectives (data-driven improvement suggestions). [DOC]

> "Principio Rector: Scrum no es un proceso — es un marco para descubrir procesos."

## Trend 2: Flow Metrics Complementing Velocity

Teams augmenting velocity with flow metrics (cycle time, throughput, WIP age) for more nuanced delivery insights. Probabilistic forecasting replacing deterministic velocity-based planning. [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 3: Product-Led Scrum

PO role evolving toward product management with emphasis on outcomes (OKRs, business metrics) rather than output (story completion). Sprint goals tied to measurable business outcomes. [DOC]

## Trend 4: Async Scrum for Distributed Teams

Ceremonies adapted for distributed/async work — async daily updates, recorded sprint reviews, and Miro-based retrospectives replacing all-synchronous Scrum events. [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 5: Scrum + DevOps Integration

Sprint boundaries becoming less rigid as CI/CD enables continuous delivery. Sprint remains the planning cadence while deployment happens continuously within and across sprints. [DOC]

| Evolution | Sprint Role | Deployment Model |
|-----------|------------|-----------------|
| Classic Scrum | Plan + deliver + review | Sprint-end release |
| Scrum + CI/CD | Plan + review cadence | Continuous deployment |
| Flow-based Scrum | Planning cadence only | On-demand release |
