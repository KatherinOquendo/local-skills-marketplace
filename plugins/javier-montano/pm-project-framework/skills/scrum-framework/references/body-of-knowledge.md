# Body of Knowledge — Scrum Framework

## 1. Definition and Scope

Scrum is a lightweight agile framework for developing, delivering, and sustaining complex products. It employs an iterative, incremental approach through fixed-length sprints, defined roles, events, and artifacts to optimize predictability and control risk. [DOC]

## 2. Foundational Frameworks

## 3. Scrum Pillars

| Pillar | Description | Mechanism |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| Transparency | Significant aspects visible to those responsible | Artifacts, Definition of Done |
| Inspection | Frequent examination of artifacts and progress | Sprint Review, Daily Scrum |
| Adaptation | Adjust process when deviation detected | Sprint Retrospective, backlog refinement |

## 4. Scrum Roles

| Role | Accountability | Key Activities |
|------|---------------|---------------|
| Product Owner | Maximize product value | Backlog management, stakeholder liaison [STAKEHOLDER] |
| Scrum Master | Scrum effectiveness | Facilitation, impediment removal, coaching |
| Developers | Creating Done increment | Design, build, test, deploy each sprint |

## 5. Scrum Events

| Event | Timebox | Purpose | Participants |
|-------|---------|---------|-------------|
| Sprint | 1-4 weeks | Container for all work | Entire team |
| Sprint Planning | 8 hours (4-week sprint) | Plan sprint work | Entire team [PLAN] |
| Daily Scrum | 15 minutes | Inspect progress, adapt plan | Developers |
| Sprint Review | 4 hours (4-week sprint) | Inspect increment, adapt backlog | Team + stakeholders |
| Sprint Retrospective | 3 hours (4-week sprint) | Inspect process, plan improvements | Entire team |

## 6. Scrum Artifacts

| Artifact | Commitment | Purpose |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| Product Backlog | Product Goal | Ordered list of everything needed |
| Sprint Backlog | Sprint Goal | Plan for delivering increment |
| Increment | Definition of Done | Usable, valuable product step [METRIC] |

## 7. Key Metrics

| Metric | Measurement | Purpose |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| Velocity | Story points per sprint | Capacity planning |
| Sprint burndown | Remaining work vs. time | Sprint progress |
| Cycle time | Start to done per item | Flow efficiency |
| Sprint goal success rate | Goals met / Total sprints | Predictability [METRIC] |
| Defect escape rate | Defects found post-sprint | Quality |
