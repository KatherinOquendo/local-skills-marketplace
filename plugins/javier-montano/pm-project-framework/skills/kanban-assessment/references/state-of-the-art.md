# State of the Art — Kanban Assessment (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: Automated Flow Analytics

Tools like ActionableAgile, Nave, and Jira's built-in analytics provide automated flow metric calculation and anomaly detection, enabling continuous Kanban assessment. [DOC]

- **Evidence**: ActionableAgile and Nave adoption growing 50%+ annually (2024-2025)
- **Impact**: Continuous assessment replaces periodic reviews; requires metric literacy
- **Maturity**: Early Majority

## Trend 2: Probabilistic Forecasting Adoption

Teams use Monte Carlo simulations based on historical flow data to provide probabilistic delivery forecasts, a key indicator of Kanban maturity level 4. [DOC]

- **Evidence**: Monte Carlo tools integrated into Jira, Azure DevOps, and standalone platforms
- **Impact**: More honest forecasting; challenges traditional commitment-based planning
- **Maturity**: Early Adopter to Early Majority

## Trend 3: Upstream Kanban Assessment

Assessment expanding beyond delivery teams to include demand management, intake processes, and portfolio-level flow. Upstream Kanban practices gaining formal assessment criteria. [INFERENCIA]

- **Evidence**: Kanban University's Upstream Kanban training and certification (2024)
- **Impact**: End-to-end flow optimization; requires cross-functional cooperation
- **Maturity**: Early Adopter

## Trend 4: AI-Driven Improvement Suggestions

AI analyzes flow data patterns and recommends specific improvements (WIP limit adjustments, policy changes, bottleneck resolution) based on similar team patterns. [INFERENCIA]

- **Evidence**: Emerging features in enterprise agile tools; research prototypes
- **Impact**: Data-driven improvement; risk of over-standardization
- **Maturity**: Innovator
