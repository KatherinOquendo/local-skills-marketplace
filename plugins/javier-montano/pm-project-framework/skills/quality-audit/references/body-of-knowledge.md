# Body of Knowledge — Quality Audit

## 1. Definition and Scope

A quality audit is a structured, independent examination of project processes, deliverables, and management systems to determine whether they comply with defined quality standards, policies, and procedures. It identifies gaps, non-conformances, and improvement opportunities. [DOC]

## 2. Foundational Frameworks

| Framework | Source | Key Contribution |
|-----------|--------|-----------------|
| ISO 9001:2015 | ISO | Quality management system requirements |
| ISO 19011:2018 | ISO | Guidelines for auditing management systems |

| Six Sigma | Motorola/GE | Defect reduction methodology |

## 3. Types of Quality Audits

| Type | Scope | Conducted By | Purpose |
|------|-------|-------------|---------|
| Internal audit | Within organization | Internal QA team | Self-assessment |
| External audit | Third-party certification | External auditor | Compliance verification |
| Process audit | Workflow and procedures | QA specialist | Process conformance |
| Product audit | Deliverables and outputs | QA/testing team | Deliverable quality |
| System audit | Entire QMS | Senior auditor | System effectiveness |

## 4. Audit Process Steps

1. **Planning** — Define scope, criteria, schedule, and audit team [PLAN]
2. **Preparation** — Review documentation, create checklists, notify auditees
3. **Execution** — Conduct interviews, observe processes, collect evidence [METRIC]
4. **Analysis** — Evaluate findings against criteria, classify non-conformances
5. **Reporting** — Document findings, observations, and recommendations
6. **Follow-up** — Verify corrective actions, close audit items [PLAN]

## 5. Non-Conformance Classification

| Severity | Definition | Response Required |
|----------|-----------|------------------|
| Critical | System failure, safety risk, legal violation | Immediate corrective action |
| Major | Significant process gap affecting quality | Corrective action within 30 days |
| Minor | Isolated deviation, low impact | Correction at next review cycle |
| Observation | Improvement opportunity, not a deviation | Optional improvement |

## 6. Audit Evidence Types

- Document review (procedures, records, logs)
- Process observation (watching work being performed)
- Interviews (structured conversations with practitioners)
- Data analysis (metrics, trends, statistical sampling)
- Testing and inspection (product examination) [METRIC]

## 7. Key Audit Metrics

| Metric | Formula | Target |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Non-conformance rate | NCRs per audit / Total audit items | < 5% |
| Corrective action closure rate | Closed CAs / Total CAs issued | ≥ 95% within SLA |
| Audit coverage | Processes audited / Total processes | 100% annually |
| Repeat finding rate | Repeat NCRs / Total NCRs | < 10% |
| Audit efficiency | Findings per audit hour | Benchmark-dependent |
