# Body of Knowledge — Project Charter

> Standards, templates, and practitioner guidance for project charter creation across methodologies.

---

## 1. Standards & Frameworks

### PMBOK 7th Edition — Project Charter

- **Relevance:** PMI defines the charter as "a document issued by the project initiator or sponsor that formally authorizes the existence of a project and provides the PM with authority to apply organizational resources."
- **Key elements:** Business need, measurable objectives, high-level requirements, high-level description, overall project risk, summary milestone schedule, preapproved financial resources, key stakeholder list, approval requirements, assigned PM with authority level, name/authority of sponsor.
- **Source:** PMI (2021). *PMBOK Guide — 7th Edition*.

### PRINCE2 — Project Brief & Project Initiation Document (PID)

- **Relevance:** PRINCE2 separates initiation into two documents: the Project Brief (equivalent to lean charter, created in Starting Up) and the PID (equivalent to full charter, created in Initiating). The PID includes the Business Case, which is the primary justification tool.
- **Key differences from PMBOK:** PRINCE2 emphasizes continued business justification (the project must remain viable throughout). The Business Case is a living document, reviewed at every stage gate.
- **Source:** AXELOS (2023). *Managing Successful Projects with PRINCE2 — 7th Edition*.

### ISO 21502:2020 — Project Management Guidance

- **Relevance:** International standard that defines project charter requirements methodology-agnostically. Emphasizes benefits realization as the ultimate purpose of the charter.
- **Source:** ISO (2020). *ISO 21502:2020 — Project, programme and portfolio management — Guidance on project management*.

## 2. Charter Sections (PMO-APEX Standard)

| Section | Purpose | Minimum Content |
|---------|---------|----------------|
| Executive Summary | 1-paragraph overview | Problem, solution approach, expected outcome |
| Business Case | Justification | Strategic alignment, benefits (quantified), opportunity cost |
| Objectives | What success looks like | ≥3 SMART objectives with KPIs |
| Scope Statement | Boundaries | Inclusions (≥5), Exclusions (≥3), Out of scope |
| Milestones | Key dates | ≥5 milestones with target dates and decision gates |
| Assumptions & Constraints | Known unknowns | Each tagged H/M/L confidence |
| Success Criteria | Acceptance test | ≥2 quantitative KPIs with measurement method |
| Governance | Authority structure | PM authority, escalation path, reporting cadence |
| Risk Preview | Early warning | Top 5 risks with preliminary responses |
| Authorization | Formal approval | Sponsor name, date, authority level |

## 3. SMART Objectives Framework

| Criterion | Test | Example Pass | Example Fail |
|-----------|------|-------------|-------------|
| **S**pecific | Describes a concrete outcome | "Reduce order processing time" | "Improve efficiency" |
| **M**easurable | Has a quantitative target | "from 48h to 24h" | "significantly" |
| **A**chievable | Within team's capability | "using existing ERP modules" | "cure cancer" |
| **R**elevant | Links to business goal | "aligned with Q3 OKR-2" | No link stated |
| **T**ime-bound | Has a deadline | "by December 2026" | "eventually" |

## 4. Charter Variants by Methodology

| Variant | Pages | Key Difference | When to Use |
|---------|-------|---------------|------------|
| **Lean Charter** | 1-2 | Vision + objectives + boundaries. No formal governance | Agile, Kanban, small projects |
| **Full PMBOK Charter** | 5-8 | All 10 sections. Formal authorization block | Waterfall, regulated, >50 FTE-months |
| **Recovery Charter** | 3-5 | Includes root cause analysis + corrective plan | Troubled projects requiring restart |
| **Program Charter** | 6-10 | Component project list + strategic alignment map | Programs, portfolios, transformations |
| **SAFe Lean Business Case** | 2-3 | Solution, MVP, investment timeline, WSJF | SAFe ARTs and solution trains |

## 5. Business Case Structure

### Cost-Benefit Analysis Template (Price-Free)

| Element | Description | Example |
|---------|------------|---------|
| Investment Required | FTE-months, infrastructure | 18 FTE-months, 2 cloud environments |
| Benefits (Quantified) | Measurable business outcomes | 50% reduction in processing time, 30% fewer errors |
| Benefits (Qualitative) | Non-measurable but valuable | Improved team morale, better stakeholder trust |
| Strategic Alignment | Which OKR/goal | OKR Q3-2: "Operational excellence" |
| Opportunity Cost | What we forgo | Delay of Feature X roadmap by 1 quarter |
| Break-Even Horizon | When benefits > investment | Month 8 post-go-live |

**NEVER include monetary values.** Use FTE-months, story points, sprints, and qualitative impact descriptions.
