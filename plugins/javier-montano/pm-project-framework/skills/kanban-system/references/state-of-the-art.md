# State of the Art — Kanban System (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: Portfolio Kanban Adoption

Organizations apply Kanban principles at the portfolio level, managing strategic initiatives as flow items with portfolio-level WIP limits and service-level expectations. [DOC]

- **Evidence**: Flight Levels framework (Klaus Leopold) adoption by enterprise PMOs
- **Impact**: Strategic flow optimization; requires executive buy-in
- **Maturity**: Early Adopter to Early Majority

## Trend 2: AI-Enhanced Flow Optimization

AI models analyze Kanban flow data to recommend WIP limit adjustments, predict bottlenecks, and suggest policy changes in real-time. [INFERENCIA]

- **Evidence**: Emerging features in ActionableAgile and enterprise platforms (2025)
- **Impact**: Continuous optimization; risk of over-automating human judgment
- **Maturity**: Innovator to Early Adopter

## Trend 3: Kanban for Knowledge Work Beyond IT

Kanban spreading to marketing, HR, legal, and finance teams. Generic Kanban frameworks replace software-specific implementations. [DOC]

- **Evidence**: Kanban University reporting 40% of new certifications from non-IT sectors (2024)
- **Impact**: Cross-functional flow management; requires domain adaptation
- **Maturity**: Early Majority

## Trend 4: Monte Carlo Forecasting as Standard

Probabilistic forecasting using Monte Carlo simulation becoming the standard Kanban forecasting method, replacing deterministic estimates. [DOC]

- **Evidence**: Tools like ActionableAgile, Nave, and Jira plugins make MC accessible
- **Impact**: More honest forecasting; cultural shift from "when will it be done?" to probability ranges
- **Maturity**: Early Majority
