# State of the Art — Risk Response Planning (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: AI-Recommended Risk Responses

AI analyzes historical project data to recommend response strategies based on what worked for similar risks in past projects. Recommendation engines suggest specific actions with effectiveness predictions. [DOC]

> "Principio Rector: Planificar la respuesta al riesgo es comprar opciones sobre el futuro."

## Trend 2: Automated Response Triggering

Risk responses pre-configured with trigger conditions that activate automatically. When KRI thresholds are breached, predefined actions execute without human intervention for routine risks. [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 3: Agile Risk Response Sprints

Dedicated capacity in each sprint for risk response actions (typically 10-15% of sprint capacity). Risk responses treated as sprint backlog items with acceptance criteria. [DOC]

## Trend 4: Response Portfolio Optimization

Instead of planning responses individually, optimizing the entire response portfolio for maximum risk reduction within budget constraints. Portfolio-level analysis identifies synergies between responses. [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 5: Resilience-Based Response Design

Shift from threat-specific responses to building organizational resilience that handles multiple risk types. Investing in capabilities (cross-training, automation, modular architecture) that reduce vulnerability broadly. [DOC]

| Approach | Focus | Investment | Flexibility |
|----------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Risk-specific responses | Individual threats | Per-risk | Low |
| Resilience building | Systemic capability | Upfront | High |
| Hybrid | Critical + systemic | Balanced | Medium |
