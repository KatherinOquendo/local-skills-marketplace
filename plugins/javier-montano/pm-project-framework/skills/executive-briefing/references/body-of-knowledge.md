# Body of Knowledge — Executive Briefing & Sponsor Updates

## Marco Teórico

El executive briefing es un instrumento de comunicación diseñado para audiencias de alta dirección que necesitan máximo insight en mínimo tiempo. Se fundamenta en el principio de "pirámide invertida": conclusión primero, evidencia después, detalle disponible por drill-down.

## Estructura del Executive Briefing (Pyramid Structure)

| Nivel | Contenido | Tiempo | Formato |
|-------|-----------|--------|---------|
| 1 - Lead | RAG status + headline | 10 segundos | Banner/header |
| 2 - Summary | 3-5 KPIs + decisions needed | 2 minutos | Dashboard cards |
| 3 - Detail | Risk analysis + strategic alignment | 3 minutos | Tables + charts |
| 4 - Appendix | Supporting data + drill-down | On-demand | Links to reports |

## RAG System para Briefings (Blue-Amber-Red)

| Status | Color | Criterio | Acción Ejecutiva |
|--------|-------|----------|-----------------|
| Healthy | Blue (#2563EB) | KPIs on track, risks managed | Ninguna — informativo |
| At Risk | Amber (#F59E0B) | ≥1 KPI below threshold, mitigation in progress | Awareness, possible support |
| Critical | Red (#DC2626) | ≥1 KPI significantly off, requires intervention | Decision needed, resource allocation |

**NUNCA usar verde.** Blue reemplaza Green en todo el sistema RAG APEX.

## Métricas Ejecutivas por Tipo de Proyecto

| Tipo | Top 5 KPIs | Frecuencia |
|------|-----------|-----------|
| Agile | Velocity, Sprint Completion, Defect Density, Team Satisfaction, Scope Completion | Per sprint/PI |
| Waterfall | CPI, SPI, EAC, Milestone Completion, Defect Rate | Per phase gate |
| Portfolio | On-time %, On-budget %, Benefits Realized, Resource Utilization, Risk Exposure | Monthly/quarterly |
| Transformation | Adoption Rate, Change Readiness, Stakeholder Satisfaction, Value Delivered, Resistance Level | Monthly |

## Decision Framing Template

```
DECISION NEEDED: [Clear statement of what needs deciding]
DEADLINE: [Date by which decision is needed]
IMPACT OF DELAY: [What happens if decision is not made by deadline]

OPTIONS:
  Option A: [Description]
    - Pro: [Benefits]
    - Con: [Risks/costs]
    - Recommendation level: [Recommended / Viable / Not Recommended]

  Option B: [Description]
    - Pro: [Benefits]
    - Con: [Risks/costs]
    - Recommendation level: [Recommended / Viable / Not Recommended]

PM RECOMMENDATION: Option [X] because [rationale in 1 sentence]
```

## Audience Calibration

| Audience | Focus | Depth | Format | Frequency |
|----------|-------|-------|--------|-----------|
| CEO/Board | Strategic alignment, ROI | Minimal | 1-page + verbal | Quarterly |
| Sponsor | Project health, decisions | Summary + detail | 2-3 pages | Bi-weekly |
| Steering Committee | Cross-project coordination | Moderate | Presentation 10-15 min | Monthly |
| VP/Director | Departmental impact | Moderate | Report 3-5 pages | Monthly |
| CTO | Technical risk, architecture | Technical depth | Technical brief | Per sprint/milestone |
| CFO | Financial performance | Financial detail | Financial dashboard | Monthly |

## Common Executive Communication Errors

| Error | Consecuencia | Corrección |
|-------|-------------|-----------|
| Leading with detail, not conclusion | Executive loses attention | Pyramid structure: conclusion first |
| Too many metrics | Information overload | Maximum 5-7 KPIs |
| No decisions framed | Meeting without outcomes | Every briefing includes decision queue |
| Burying bad news | Surprise when discovered later | Red items lead the briefing |
| Only reporting, no forecasting | Reactive management | Include forecast and trajectory |
| Generic, not personalized | Low engagement | Calibrate to audience priorities |
