# State of the Art — Budget Baseline (2024-2026)

## Trend 1: AI-Assisted Cost Estimation

ML models trained on historical project cost data improve estimation accuracy by 20-30%. Parametric models auto-calibrate based on organizational complexity factors. [DOC]

- **Accuracy improvement**: From Class 3 to Class 2 estimates automatically [METRIC]
- **Tools**: Forecast.app, custom regression models on project databases [DOC]

## Trend 2: Rolling-Wave Budgeting in Agile

Traditional fixed baselines give way to rolling-wave approaches where near-term sprints have detailed budgets and future sprints have rough-order-magnitude allocations. [DOC]

- **Pattern**: Detailed budget for 3 sprints + ROM for remaining backlog [PLAN]
- **Challenge**: EVM integration with variable baselines [INFERENCIA]

## Trend 3: Real-Time Budget Tracking

Cloud-based PM tools provide real-time budget consumption dashboards replacing monthly variance reports. Burn rate alerts trigger before overruns occur. [DOC]

- **Tools**: Harvest, Float, custom Power BI dashboards [DOC]
- **Benefit**: 2-3 week earlier detection of budget deviations [METRIC]

## Trend 4: Probabilistic Baselines

Monte Carlo simulation replaces single-point baselines with probability distributions. Sponsors approve at specific confidence levels (P50, P80). [DOC]

- **Adoption**: 40% of large programs use probabilistic baselines [INFERENCIA]
- **Tool support**: Primavera Risk Analysis, @RISK, Crystal Ball [DOC]

## Trend 5: Carbon-Aware Budget Baselines

ESG requirements drive inclusion of carbon cost estimates alongside financial budgets. Cloud workload carbon calculators feed into project baselines. [DOC]

- **Regulation**: EU Corporate Sustainability Reporting Directive [DOC]
- **Impact**: 5-15% additional budget consideration for sustainability [SUPUESTO]

## Forecast

By 2026, budget baselines will be dynamic, probabilistic, and AI-calibrated. Static Excel-based baselines will be replaced by real-time, risk-adjusted budget models. [INFERENCIA]
